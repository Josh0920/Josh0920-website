A Day of Giving

Joshua M. Haub · December 25, 2023

Christmas is a day of giving. And the gift that started it all was announced this way:

"She will bear a Son; and you shall call His name Jesus, for He will save His people from their sins." (Matthew 1:21)

That is the gift. Not comfort, not prosperity, not a better life in the worldly sense. Salvation from sin. The thing we needed most, the thing we could not provide for ourselves — that is what God gave.

Paul says it plainly in Galatians 2:20: Christ "loved me and gave Himself up for me." The gift was not a thing. It was a Person, given entirely. The incarnation was not God sending something down. It was God coming down Himself.

We celebrate that today. And rightly so. But Christmas cannot be the whole of it. The giving cannot stop here.

Jesus gave His disciples their final instruction before ascending:

"Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I commanded you." (Matthew 28:19–20)

We received the greatest gift in history. Now we give it away. Not once a year, not in December when the culture is already thinking about it. Every day, to every person we have access to.

The gift is too good to keep to yourself. Give the Gospel — not just today, but all year long.

To Him Be the Glory.

Joshua M. Haub · December 25, 2023
