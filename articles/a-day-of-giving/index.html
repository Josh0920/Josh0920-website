A Day of Giving

Joshua M. Haub · December 25, 2023

Alas, the day is here. The day in which many have anticipated. The day children long for. The day parents’ wallets despise. The day in which many will not only receive gifts, but give. The day in which we express our thankfulness to one another. The day in which we celebrate: Christmas.

May we always remember to guard against the dangers of materialism. May we always cherish the memories we make while we spend time with loved ones.

During this time of year, as many have already pointed out, a sizeable portion of the world remember Jesus’ birth. Today, symbolically, has been culturally-deemed “Jesus’ birthday.”

While we may be quick to say, “Jesus’ death has much greater significance than His birth.” Let us remember the promise that came from just before His birth:

“‘She [Mary] will bear a Son; you shall call His name Jesus, for He will save His people from their sins’” (Matthew 1:21, NASB1995).

There is no greater gift ever given than this promise. Jesus’ gift of forgiveness is the gift to celebrate, not just on Christmas, but every day of our lives. If we only celebrate Christ on “His birthday,” we have missed the point of His coming. The day Jesus was born is of no great significance, otherwise the Spirit would have guaranteed that the date would have been recorded.

May I suggest that perhaps part of the reason the date of His birth was never mentioned was to make sure we do not become attached to only celebrating Him on one particular day of the year.

We, as God’s people, do not just celebrate His birth, but His death, and His life. We celebrate Jesus. We celebrate Him every day. We thank God for the gift of Matthew 1:21 every day. We live knowing that Jesus truly has saved us from our sins.

As a child, in Bible class, we used to recite this phrase: “Before the world began, God had a great plan.” That is precisely what we ought to remember. God has given us the greatest gift, that “while we were yet sinners [verse 10 calls us ‘enemies’] Christ died for us” (Romans 5:8, 10, NASB1995).

There is no greater gift than Christ being born and dying for us.

The greatest gift you could ever give God is your life, and the greatest gift you could ever give someone else is the Gospel.

To give God your life, you must die to yourself. You must give up your life and dreams in order to find eternal life (Mark 9:35). Jesus demands complete obedience. We must give up our desires so much so that we become as Paul was: “…It is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me…” (Galatians 2:20, NASB1995). We die in Baptism (Romans 6:4). We die so Christ can reign supreme in our lives.

Finish the verse: “and the life I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved ME and gave Himself up for ME” (Galatians 2:20, NASB1995). Christ died for us all. Paul made that personal. Christ didn’t just die for all mankind, but for you. Live life knowing Christ died for YOU.

I cannot help but interject this amazing section: “Therefore remember, that formerly you, the Gentiles in the flesh… remember that you were at that time separate from Christ, excluded from the commonwealth of Israel, and strangers to the covenants of promise, having no hope and without God in the world. But now in Christ Jesus you who formerly were far off have been brought near by the blood of Christ” (Eph 2:11–13, NASB1995). I don’t know about all of you, but I am a Gentile. I’m not a Jew. Paul says I had no hope! I was lost in paganism! I wasn’t a part of God’s special people! I was a goner! Yet, Christ brought ME, personally, a gift. How could I ever thank Him enough for including a filthy Gentile like me in His Kingdom?

If we dare try to live a double life (partly for Christ, partly for this world), we will fail. Christ said, “‘No one can serve two masters; for either he will hate the one and love the other, or he will be devoted to one and despise the other. You cannot serve God and wealth’” (Matthew 6:24).

God has given us both Christ and His Word; how are you showing your appreciation for those gifts? Jesus told the 11: “‘Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I commanded you; and lo, I am with you always, even to the end of the age’” (Matthew 28:19–20). How are you doing with that?

While we celebrate Christmas together, let us never forget the gift God has given us that far exceeds the gifts we give today, and remember to give year-round the Gift that God has commissioned for us to give: The Gospel.

“‘She [Mary] will bear a Son; you shall call His name Jesus, for He will save His people from their sins’” (Matthew 1:21, NASB1995).

I will leave you with one simple question: Are you a part of “His people”? And, if you are not, what are you doing about that?

To Him be the Glory.

Joshua M. Haub · December 25, 2023
