Love Like He Did

Joshua M. Haub · February 14, 2024

Today is a special day. Today, we celebrate those whom we love. Some will celebrate it with a special someone. Others, with family. Still others, with friends. While gifts, flowers, and chocolates are a staple of Valentine’s Day, the celebration of others runs deeper than any sweets or flowers could last. Possessions die, rot, and waste away, but love endures.

I am reminded of what the Gospel of John says about God:

“For God so loved the world, that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him shall not perish, but have eternal life” (John 3:16, NASB1995).

God so LOVED that He did what?

I believe so often, when it comes to holidays (especially birthdays and Christmas), we desire to increase our possessions. We are excited, not because we are able to give, but because we are able to get.

Yet, God showed His love by giving. Love gives. God so loved the world that He GAVE the greatest gift anyone could ever give. His only Son. Because He loved, we could turn and be saved. No flower could smell better to God than us walking in love (Ephesians 5:1–2).

While John 3:16 is probably the most well known verse of all time, 1 John 3:16 has something important for us, as Believers:

“We know love by this, that He laid down His life for us; and we ought to lay down our lives for the brethren” (1 John 3:16).

While John 3:16 focuses on how God loved us, 1 John 3:16 shows us how Jesus loved us. He loved enough to die. Christ is our model of love. We ought to be willing to die for our brethren. Notice, the implication of “for the brethren” is for ALL our brethren. I think we tend to accept the fact that there are a few people we would be willing to die for: family, close friends, and those we especially love. But, what about that “one guy” at church who has become a sort of burden in your life? If we are not willing to die for ALL our brethren, we fall short of this passage. I am sure dying for Pharisees and Scribes was not particularly appealing to the Man, Jesus. Yet, He desired all men to be saved (1 Timothy 2:4).

Christ is our model. He came as an example that we should follow in His footsteps (1 John 2:6).

Just days before Jesus was crucified, He spent time in private with His disciples. In the midst of His final hours with them, He said,

“‘This is My commandment, that you love one another, just as I have loved you. Greater love has no one than this, that one lay down his life for his friends. You are My friends if you do what I command you’” (John 15:12–14).

It all connects, doesn’t it? If I love my brother, then I am a friend of Christ. And if my friend loves me, He is friend of Christ too. We, then, are united together with Him. Yet, the middle verse, v. 13, is the hardest example of them all. It connects with the end of v. 12, “Just as I have loved you.” To love as Christ did means being willing to die for your friends. Being willing to die is a difficult call, yet it is one we must be willing to accept.

If we are to be like Christ, perhaps there is no greater text than Philippians 2:

“Therefore if there is any encouragement in Christ, if there is any consolation of love, if there is any fellowship of the Spirit, if any affection and compassion, make my joy complete by being of the same mind, maintaining the same love, united in spirit, intent on one purpose. Do nothing from selfishness or empty conceit, but with humility of mind regard one another as more important than yourselves; do not merely look out for your own personal interests, but also for the interests of others. Have this attitude in yourselves which was also in Christ Jesus, who, although He existed in the form of God, did not regard equality with God a thing to be grasped, but emptied Himself, taking the form of a bond-servant, and being made in the likeness of men. Being found in appearance as a man, He humbled Himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross” (Philippians 2:1–8).

How can you be like Christ? Humble yourselves, be united, serve God fully, and put others above self. In other words, die to yourself and your own desires and live in obedience to God and love for your brother.

What does it mean to love? It means to have the mind of Christ.

Have you been loving like Christ?

To Him be the Glory.
