Reflection

Love Like He Did

Joshua M. Haub · February 14, 2024

Valentine's Day. The world has its version of love — feelings, romance, sentimentality. Scripture has a different definition, and it is far more demanding.

John 3:16 is the most familiar verse in the Bible. Read it slowly:

"For God so loved the world, that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him shall not perish, but have eternal life."

God loved — and He gave. The love is defined by the giving. It is not a feeling God had toward us. It is an action He took at enormous cost.

John comes back to this in his first letter, and he makes the implication explicit:

"We know love by this, that He laid down His life for us; and we ought to lay down our lives for the brethren." (1 John 3:16)

Same chapter number, different book. The verse we know by heart tells us God gave His Son. The verse we rarely quote tells us what that means for how we live. We know what love is because He showed us — and having seen it, we are called to do likewise.

Paul describes the mind of Christ in Philippians 2: He did not grasp His position. He emptied Himself. He became a servant. He became obedient to death.

That is the shape of love. It does not demand. It gives. It does not protect itself. It lays itself down.

Love the people in your life like He loved us. Be willing to lay it all down for them.

To Him Be the Glory.
