Reflection

Outside the Camp

Joshua M. Haub · August 22, 2025

“For the bodies of those animals whose blood is brought into the holy place by the high priest as an offering for sin, are burned outside the camp. Therefore Jesus also, that He might sanctify the people through His own blood, suffered outside the gate. So, let us go out to Him outside the camp, bearing His reproach. For here we do not have a lasting city, but we are seeking the city which is to come.” (Hebrews 13:11–14, NASB95)

Let’s go out to Him, bearing His reproach. If they persecuted Jesus, they are surely going to treat us the same way… but we go out to Him outside the camp and leave our past way of life. We are traveling to God’s heavenly city.

Here’s the image: “You can make fun of me all you want. You can gossip about me. You can lie about me. You can persecute me. You can post lies about me on social media. You can take my stuff. You can stop being my friend. You can fire me from my job. You can hurt me. You can point your fingers at me. You can call me weird, dumb, small-minded, controlled by religion, a sheep. You said the same things about my Lord, so it really doesn’t matter if you say the same things about me. I’ve left my old life where I lived like you, and now I am seeking for God’s city.”

To Him be the Glory.

Joshua M. Haub · August 22, 2025
