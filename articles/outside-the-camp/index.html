Reflection

Outside the Camp

Joshua M. Haub · August 22, 2025

Under the Old Covenant, when an animal was sacrificed for sin, its body was taken outside the camp and burned. It could not remain inside. It bore, in a physical sense, what did not belong in the holy place.

The writer of Hebrews draws a direct line between that practice and what happened to Jesus:

"For the bodies of those animals whose blood is brought into the holy place by the high priest as an offering for sin, are burned outside the camp. Therefore Jesus also, that He might sanctify the people through His own blood, suffered outside the gate." (Hebrews 13:11–12)

Outside the gate. He was taken out of the city, to Golgotha, and crucified there. Excluded. Placed where the waste was burned. That is where He died.

And then the writer says something startling:

"So, let us go out to Him outside the camp, bearing His reproach. For here we do not have a lasting city, but we are seeking the city which is to come." (Hebrews 13:13–14)

Let us go out to Him. Not invite Him into our comfortable place. Go out to where He is — outside, bearing reproach.

The readers of this letter were being pressured to return to Judaism, to the safety and social respectability of the old system. The writer is saying: do not go back inside the camp. Jesus is not there. Go out to where He is, even though it costs you.

We are not looking for a lasting city here. We are looking for the one that is to come. That changes the calculus of comfort. If this world is not our permanent address, why are we so determined to be comfortable in it? Why are we so afraid of standing where Jesus stood — outside, excluded, bearing reproach?

Go out to Him. That is where He is.

To Him Be the Glory.
