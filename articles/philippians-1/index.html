Reflection

Philippians 1

Joshua M. Haub · August 17, 2024

Chapter 1:

Paul writes to the church in Philippi which struggled greatly with unity. With the threat of Judaizing teachers (ch. 3) either within the church already or a possible threat, Paul writes Philippians from his jail in Rome. Chapter 1, Paul focuses on his situation in which he refuses to dwell on himself and his depressing situation and points to the joy he finds in Christ and others. Paul’s in prison, but he’s rejoicing that the Gospel is being spread. People are trying to hurt Paul, but he rejoices that Christ is being taught. Paul may die for his faith, but he desperately wants Christ to be boldly glorified whether he lives or dies. Paul’s life is 100% about Christ, and that is seen through this entire section. Paul knows that dying and going to be with Christ is better than living, but he wants to stay to help the Philippians in their faith. Paul is the embodiment of Luke 9:23–26 and Gal 2:20.

Why does Paul write about his situation and about his attitude/focus? Was it to boast? No, it was to point the Philippians to the next section: “Conduct yourselves worth of the Gospel…” (1:27). The Philippians needed to follow Paul’s example. The Philippians needed to stop focusing on themselves and unite together as one. By uniting, they would be strong enough to deal with false teaching (1:28, ch. 3) and fix their unity problems (ch. 2, 4). Paul gives many examples for the Philippians to follow: himself (ch. 1, 2, 3, 4), Christ (ch. 2), Timothy and Epaphroditus (ch. 2), and faithful Christians (ch. 3).

“I have been crucified with Christ; and it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me; and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave Himself up for me.” (Galatians 2:20, NASB95)

Are we crucified with Christ like Paul was?

To Him be the Glory.
