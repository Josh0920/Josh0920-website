Struggle with God

Joshua M. Haub · September 9, 2023

There is a character that stands out in Israel’s history: Jacob. Jacob is one of the forefathers of the nation of Israel. In fact, God changed Jacob’s name to Israel, the name his descendants took to be their nation’s name. Jacob has an interesting story. In Genesis 32, Jacob has an encounter with an angel. While Jacob is preparing to meet his older brother, whom he believes is still angry about him stealing his birthright and blessing from, an angel comes and begins to wrestle with him.

Jacob and this angel wrestle “until daybreak” (32:24). Jacob put up a good fight, even against an angel! The angel ended up dislocating his thigh by touching it, yet Jacob still wouldn’t let go! The angel even told Jacob to let him go, but Jacob was unwilling. Jacob says, “‘I will not let you go unless you bless me’” (vs 26). After changing Jacob’s name, the angel blesses him.

Jacob had spent his life, up until this point, trying to do things his way. Earlier, Jacob prayed to God saying, “God… if You… then I will…” (cf. 28:20–22). God responds to this prayer in 31:11–13, telling Jacob to go back home. God was going to keep His end of Jacob’s deal, the question was: would Jacob keep his?

How often do we want to do things our way? How often do we pray, “God, if You do ___ for me, then I will serve You!”

Maybe we don’t say it verbally, but is that our attitude?

In Genesis 32, Jacob finally comes face to face with reality. He has to wrestle with God. If we are being honest, Jacob had been wrestling with God the whole time, but now, he literally does. There is a special lesson to be learned from Jacob’s wrestling with God.

#1 — Jacob never gave up. Even though God wounded Jacob (dislocating his thigh), Jacob wouldn’t stop wrestling God. He had to keep wrestling. He could’ve stopped, but he didn’t. Why? Because he needed God.

Are we willing to wrestle with God, even when it hurts us? Why do we persist in faith when it hurts? Because we can’t do this life without Him. No matter how bad it hurts us, are we determined that we cannot do this without Him?

#2 — Jacob wanted a blessing. This blessing was not what we would immediately think. Jacob was not wanting God to give him something, but rather, that God would be with him. Now, Jacob wants God’s help, not just His blessing (cf. 28:20–22).

Do you want God to be with you? Jacob came to the realization, “I can’t do this without God!” Are you of the same mind?

In the words of a dear friend of mine (speaking upon this story): “How often do we wrestle with God to see who’s in control of our life? We want to control it by our plans, by our ideas, by our will, when God needs to cripple us and make us realize it’s Him and not us.”

Are you willing to hold onto God, begging Him to be with you?

Become an Israel:

“He said, ‘Your name shall no longer be Jacob [lit. Supplanter], but Israel; for you have striven with God and with men and have prevailed.’” (Genesis 32:28, NASB1995)
“So Jacob named the place Peniel, for he said, ‘I have seen God face to face, yet my life has been preserved.’” (Genesis 32:30)

Perhaps you need to come to a place of Peniel (Lit. “Face of God”) in which you look at God and say, “God, I need You. I can’t do this without You.”

The Bible says in verse 31 that Jacob crossed over the Penuel and was “limping on his thigh.” Sometimes, facing God gives you scars. God kills our self-reliance. Pride, self-righteousness, self-sufficiency, etc are all destroyed under servitude to Christ. But the truth is that limping with faith in God is better than walking without it.

This story asks us the question: will you struggle with God for a blessing, or will you let go of God and rely on your own abilities to handle the situations and difficulties of life?

Hold on, as Israel did.

To Him be the glory.
