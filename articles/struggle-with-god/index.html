Struggle With God

Joshua M. Haub · September 9, 2023

Jacob is alone at the ford of Jabbok, the night before he meets Esau. He has sent his family and possessions ahead. And then something unexpected happens:

"And a man wrestled with him until daybreak." (Genesis 32:24)

All night. Until daybreak. Jacob wrestles with this man — who is God in human form — through the entire dark stretch of hours before dawn. At some point the man touches Jacob's hip and dislocates it. Jacob is now wrestling at a disadvantage, in pain, barely able to stand.

And he will not let go.

"Then he said, 'Let me go, for the dawn is breaking.' But he said, 'I will not let you go unless you bless me.'" (Genesis 32:26)

Even injured, even exhausted, even when told to release his grip — Jacob holds on. He will not give up until he has received a blessing. That is not defiance. That is desperate, persistent faith.

His name is changed to Israel — "he who strives with God." He names the place Peniel: "I have seen God face to face, yet my life has been preserved." And then he crosses the ford, limping.

He limped for the rest of his life. The encounter left a permanent mark. But he carried a blessing with it.

There are seasons when following God feels like a struggle — when prayer feels like wrestling in the dark, when the answers do not come, when the hip gets touched and you can barely stand. The question is whether you let go or hold on.

Limping with faith in God is better than walking without it. Will you struggle with God until He blesses you?

To Him Be the Glory.
