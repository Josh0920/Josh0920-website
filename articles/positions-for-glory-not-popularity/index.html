Reflection

Positions for Glory, Not Popularity

Joshua M. Haub · February 17, 2024

The mother of James and John came to Jesus with a request. She wanted her sons seated at His right and left in the kingdom — the two positions of highest honor.

Jesus did not rebuke her. He redirected her:

"You do not know what you are asking. Are you able to drink the cup that I am about to drink?" (Matthew 20:22)

They said they were able. Jesus said they would indeed drink it — but the positions themselves were not His to give. They belong to those for whom they have been prepared by the Father.

The other ten disciples heard about this and were indignant. Not because they were more righteous — but because they wanted the same thing and someone else had asked first. So Jesus gathered them all and said:

"Whoever wishes to become great among you shall be your servant, and whoever wishes to be first among you shall be your slave; just as the Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give His life a ransom for many." (Matthew 20:26–28)

The world defines greatness by position, platform, and the number of people serving you. Jesus inverts it entirely. Greatness is measured by how many you serve, not how many serve you.

The cup Jesus asked about was the cup of suffering. James and John would drink it — James by execution, John by exile. Positions in the kingdom come through the path of the cross, not around it.

Whatever position you hold — in the church, in your family, in your community — use it to serve. That is the only use of position that honors God.

To Him Be the Glory.
