Whose Approval Do We Love More?

Joshua M. Haub · October 7, 2023

John records something remarkable at the end of Jesus' public ministry. Many of the rulers — the religious leaders, the people with status and power — actually believed in Him. But they would not say so:

"Nevertheless many even of the rulers believed in Him, but because of the Pharisees they were not confessing Him, for fear that they would be put out of the synagogue; for they loved the approval of men rather than the approval of God." (John 12:42–43)

They believed. And they said nothing. Because the cost of saying something — being put out of the synagogue, losing their standing, facing the disapproval of their peers — was a cost they were not willing to pay.

John names what drove that silence: they loved the approval of men rather than the approval of God. It was not confusion. It was not unbelief. It was love — misplaced love, love given to the wrong audience.

Jesus had said something about this just before:

"He who loves his life loses it, and he who hates his life in this world will keep it to life eternal." (John 12:25)

The rulers were trying to keep their lives — their positions, their reputations, their place in the community. And in doing so, they were losing what actually mattered.

This is not an ancient problem. It lives in every church, in every heart that knows what is true and goes quiet when speaking would cost something. What we love is revealed by what we are willing to risk.

Whose approval do you love more?

To Him Be the Glory.
