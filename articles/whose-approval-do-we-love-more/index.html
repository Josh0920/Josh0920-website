Whose Approval Do We Love More?

Joshua M. Haub · October 7, 2023

In a recent study with a friend, we have been studying through John 12 and we’ve found some very impactful and important lessons. The one I wish to point out now is found in verses 42–43.

“Nevertheless many even of the rulers believed in Him, but because of the Pharisees they were not confessing Him, for fear that they would be put out of the synagogue; for they loved the approval of men rather than the approval of God.” (John 12:42–43)

This section is so important and impactful. Think about what John is trying to get across to the readers. Some rulers, people with influence, are believing in Jesus. That’s great! Perhaps they can influence other Jews to follow Him! They have a great platform to be able to proclaim Jesus from. This could begin a whole new pro-Jesus movement!

Compared to the masses, most of those who were around Jesus did not believe in Him. They rejected Him, which shows a deep heart problem. John, then, quotes Isaiah 53:1 and Isaiah 6:10, thus equating Jesus’ ministry with Isaiah’s plus the people of Jesus’ day and the people of Isaiah’s. Thus only a comparable few did believe Him.

Yet, what’s the problem John points out concerning these rulers in verse 42? They are afraid. Why? Because they cared more about what others thought than what God thought.

Isn’t that so often our problem today?

Whose approval are we seeking? God’s or Man’s?

The truth is that these rulers cared more about their positions in society (and the perks that came with it!) than they did about proclaiming Jesus.

What about us? Whose approval matters to us?

Perhaps I am only speaking to myself, but I believe I’ve put way too much merit in what the Enemy thinks about me than I have in what my Friend does.

How does that make any sense? I care more about my enemies thinking well of me than those “on my team”? They are my enemies for a reason! If they begin to like me, what does that say about me?

Imagine you’re competing in a game. Do you really care what the opponent thinks? Of course not! In fact, you probably really like it when they hate you because there is a reason that they do.

Why do we treat spiritual things any differently? When the World and Satan hates us, good. Very good. That means we must be doing something right!

Forget about what the world thinks; care more about what God thinks. And if that causes you to lose some things in this life, very very good.

“‘He who loves his life loses it, and he who hates his life in this world will keep it to life eternal.’” John 12:25

To Him be the glory.
