Be Anxious for Nothing

Joshua M. Haub · August 3, 2024

Paul gives one of the clearest commands in the New Testament — and it is also one of the most frequently ignored:

"Be anxious for nothing, but in everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known to God. And the peace of God, which surpasses all comprehension, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus." (Philippians 4:6–7)

Nothing. Not "be anxious for less." Not "try not to worry too much." Nothing. The command is absolute.

And the alternative is not positive thinking or stoic resolve. It is prayer — specific, thankful, persistent prayer. You take what you are tempted to worry about, and you bring it to God instead. You make your requests known. And then His peace, which does not make logical sense, guards your heart.

Then Paul continues:

"Finally, brethren, whatever is true, whatever is honorable, whatever is right, whatever is pure, whatever is lovely, whatever is of good repute, if there is any excellence and if anything worthy of praise, dwell on these things." (Philippians 4:8)

Anxiety is not just a feeling — it is a thought pattern. It is the mind running on a track of worst-case scenarios, returning again and again to what might go wrong. Paul interrupts that track and redirects it. Think about what is true. What is honorable. What is pure. What is good.

We spend enormous energy worrying about things that are trivial, things that will not matter in five years, things entirely outside our control. And we neglect the one place we can actually direct our minds.

Stop dwelling on what is wrong. Start dwelling on what is true.

To Him Be the Glory.
