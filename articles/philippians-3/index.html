Reflection

Philippians 3

Joshua M. Haub · October 12, 2024

Chapter 3:

“A lot people live life in reverse. They are always thinking about the past” (a dear friend of mine).

Philippians 3 is probably the most powerful chapter in the book to me.

Paul continues building 1:27–28. He speaks about 1:27 and unity (ch. 2) and now moves on to “your opponents” (1:28) in ch. 3. The false teachers taught that for you to be saved, you had to become a Jew first. Paul uses himself as an example.

Paul was among the greatest Jews of his day. His resume speaks for itself:

  • Circumcised
  • Israelite
  • Benjaminite
  • Devoted Jew
  • Pharisee
  • Persecutor of the church
  • Blameless

If any of these false teachers were going to be impressed with Judaism, they would be impressed with Paul!

And yet, Paul writes, “But whatever things were gain to me, those things I have counted as loss for the sake of Christ” (3:7).

Paul’s life was totally changed by the gospel. His life was all about growing in Christ and gaining eternal life.

In fact, Paul writes, “…but one thing I do: forgetting what lies behind and reaching forward to what lies ahead, I press on toward the goal for the price of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus” (3:13–14).

Paul let go of his past. Paul listed many accomplishments, yet he mentioned one of his greatest failures: a persecutor of the church.

Do you think Paul regretted that? Do you think Paul lived with guilt?

Some of us never learn to let go of the past.

Are there good things in our past? Yes. But, there are also some bad things.

Paul let everything go and pressed forward.

We must let the past go, let go of the guilt, and press forward.

You cannot serve God well if you continue to hold onto the past.

We’ve all made mistakes. Press forward.

“…FORGETTING…” (3:13)

To Him be the Glory.
