Reflection

Commentaries and Scripture

Joshua M. Haub · June 13, 2025

Commentaries are so helpful in understanding the Bible. I quite often will look up the “Scholarly take” on various passages as I study to see what others are seeing/saying about them. My Bible knowledge has become a lot deeper now because of these extra-Biblical writings.

However, we must be careful not to become overly impressed or persuaded by arguments made by scholars just because they “sound smart” or have a degree with hundreds of hours worth of studying the Bible. Just because someone wrote it in a book, or said it in a pulpit, doesn’t mean it is true!

Jesus (very) often criticized the scholars of His day. The Pharisees were the “experts.” They were the guys who “sounded smart” and “knew their stuff.” However, they completely missed many of His fundamental teachings.

In fact, a lot of times the common people understood Jesus better and the Pharisees needed to be taught by them (John 9:34).

Of course, there is a difference between the unbelieving Pharisees and the believing scholars (although, some scholars hold beliefs that make them essentially unbelievers!). And, I actually really like some scholars’ works. Often, I tend to agree with some that I read. But, the point still stands, what we need to do is not run to the Experts when we want to know God’s word. Instead, we need to “examine the Scriptures daily to see whether these things [are] so” (Acts 17:11).

We do not follow preachers, elders, scholars, etc. They are simply teachers. We follow Jesus Christ.

And when Jesus and Experts disagree;
when Jesus and the Experts teach different things;
when Jesus says we are saved by Grace through Obedient Faith and Experts say we are saved by Faith Only;
when Jesus says to Sing and Experts say “you get to worship however you want;”
when Jesus says to be Baptized and Experts say “pray the sinner’s prayer;”
we follow Jesus every single time.

To Him be the Glory.

Joshua M. Haub · June 13, 2025
