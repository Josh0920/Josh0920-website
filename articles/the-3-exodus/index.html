The 3 Exodus

Joshua M. Haub · February 3, 2024

There are three exodus stories in Scripture, and they run in parallel. Once you see the pattern, you cannot unsee it.

The first exodus is Israel's. They left Egypt through the Red Sea, spent forty years in the wilderness, and entered the Promised Land of Canaan. This is the story we all know — the plagues, the crossing, the manna, the water from the rock, the complaining, the covenant, and finally the inheritance.

The second exodus is Jesus'. Matthew 2:15 quotes Hosea 11:1: "Out of Egypt I called My Son." Joseph takes Mary and the child to Egypt to escape Herod, and then brings Him back — recapitulating Israel's national story in the life of one person. The first five chapters of Matthew deliberately mirror the Pentateuch: birth narrative parallels Exodus, the baptism in the Jordan parallels the Red Sea crossing, forty days in the wilderness parallels forty years, and the Sermon on the Mount parallels Moses receiving the Law on Sinai.

Jesus is the true Israel, doing what Israel failed to do — perfectly faithful through every test.

The third exodus is ours. Baptism is our Red Sea — the moment we pass through water from slavery into freedom. The Christian life is our wilderness — the journey between salvation and the Promised Land, full of trials and the need for daily dependence on God. Heaven is our Canaan — the inheritance that is waiting, the rest that remains.

"Out of Egypt I called My Son." (Matthew 2:15, quoting Hosea 11:1)

We are in the wilderness right now. We have crossed the sea. We have not yet entered the rest. Keep walking.

To Him Be the Glory.
