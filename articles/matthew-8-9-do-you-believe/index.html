Matthew 8–9 — Do You Believe?

Joshua M. Haub · December 7, 2024

Matthew 8 and 9 are a concentrated series of miracles. Jesus heals a leper, the centurion's servant, Peter's mother-in-law, two demon-possessed men, a paralytic, a woman with a hemorrhage, a ruler's daughter, two blind men, and a mute man. Miracle after miracle, almost without pause.

But running through all of it is a sharp contrast. Faith on one side. Disbelief on the other. The centurion — a Gentile — demonstrates greater faith than anyone in Israel. The disciples panic in the storm even with Jesus in the boat. The crowd from Gadarene asks Jesus to leave after He casts out the demons. The scribes accuse Him of blasphemy when He forgives sin.

Then come the two blind men. They follow Him, calling out. Jesus asks them a direct question:

"Do you believe that I am able to do this?" (Matthew 9:28)

Not "do you want to be healed?" Not "do you feel worthy?" He asks about belief. Specifically: do you believe I am able?

They say yes. And He heals them.

That question does not stay in the first century. It reaches across the page and lands on us. Whatever it is you are carrying, whatever need you are bringing — Jesus is asking the same thing. Do you believe He is able?

We have to answer it.

To Him Be the Glory.
