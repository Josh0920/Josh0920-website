Speaking the Truth in Love

Joshua M. Haub · November 25, 2023

Paul gives us a phrase that is easy to quote and hard to live:

"But speaking the truth in love, we are to grow up in all aspects into Him who is the head, even Christ." (Ephesians 4:15)

Truth in love. Both together. Not one at the expense of the other.

There are two ways to fail this. Both are common, and both do real damage.

The first failure is all love, no truth. This is the person who never says anything that might offend. They affirm everything, challenge nothing, and call it kindness. But what they are actually doing is refusing to care enough to be honest. If a friend is walking toward a cliff and you say nothing because you don't want to create tension, that is not love. It is abandonment dressed up as niceness.

The second failure is all truth, no love. This is the person who is technically correct and relationally destructive. They say the right thing in the wrong way, with the wrong spirit, at the wrong time — and use truth as a weapon rather than a gift. The person on the receiving end walks away wounded rather than helped.

Neither of these is what Paul describes. Speaking the truth in love means you care enough about the person to tell them what is real, and you care enough to do it in a way that can actually be received.

Love does not avoid truth. Love is truth-telling — done gently, done humbly, done at the right moment, done by someone who has earned the right to speak.

Grow up into Christ. That means both: truth and love, together, every time.

To Him Be the Glory.
