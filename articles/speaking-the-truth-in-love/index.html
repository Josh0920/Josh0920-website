Speaking the Truth in Love

Joshua M. Haub · November 25, 2023

“Speaking The Truth In Love” (Ephesians 4:15)

Paul told the Ephesian brethren, in the context of individual gifts/roles used to build up a congregation, to “speak the truth in love” (4:15). In today’s world, we love to promote “Love.” Culture tells us to love everyone, love this thing, love that thing, love love love! “Love” is a great thing, when used in the Biblical sense. One of the problems with the world’s definition of “Love” today is this: Love means tolerance.

This idea of “Love means tolerance” has filtered into the religious world in which people claim “everyone’s interpretations and beliefs are equally valid!” Well, not quite. In some things, sure, you can have personal opinions on, but to say things like Salvation are “up to your own interpretation” or “however you want to worship God is fine” are out of the question.

A lot of times the way Biblically-sound Christians fight against this idea of “Love means tolerance” in the religious world is by going to the other extreme and saying “Here’s the truth and you better deal with it!” To use Paul’s terminology: They speak the truth, yet without love.

These tend to be the 2 extremes: “all love and no truth” or “all truth and no love.”

(An aside: how can you truly love someone and NOT tell them the truth?)

Paul says to “speak the truth IN love.” It requires both.

Do not become so hooked on one extreme or the other. Sometimes we are tempted to emphasis the verse like this: “speak the truth IN LOVE.”

Or perhaps we emphasis it like this: “SPEAK THE TRUTH in love.”

Yet, Paul said “SPEAK THE TRUTH IN LOVE.”

How are you doing with that?

Perhaps we are too scared of offending people that we focus on the “In love” part.

Perhaps we are too heartless (and even prideful) to realize people need help, so we focus on the “Speak the truth” part.

(Another aside: Do not let your ego get in the way of people accepting the truth.)

If we want to be effective in building up our brethren, we must do both.

Our attitude must be this: I love, thus I speak the truth.

We must find the balance.

The question, then, is this: Which extreme are you?

To Him be the glory.
