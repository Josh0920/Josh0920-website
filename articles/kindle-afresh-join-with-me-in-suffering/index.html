Kindle Afresh / Join with Me in Suffering

Joshua M. Haub · April 8, 2023

“…kindle afresh…[and]…join with me in suffering for the gospel…” (parts of 2 Timothy 1:6 and 8 NASB)

—2 Timothy 1:6–12, NASB1995—

In Paul’s second letter to Timothy, Paul begins this letter to his “beloved son” (vs 2) by speaking about his thankfulness to God for him and how he is mindful of his deep devotion for Him. As Paul considers Timothy’s devotion, he reminds Timothy to “…kindle afresh the gift of God…” (vs 6). In his first letter, Paul tells Timothy to “not neglect” that gift (cf. 1 Timothy 4:14). It appears that Timothy had either needed more encouragement, or he had listened to Paul’s first exhortation, but started to neglect it again.

Why? What caused Timothy to neglect his gift (or, allow it to dim)? Consider what Paul says next. The reason why Timothy had allowed his gift to go unused was because, evidently, he was scared. Being a young evangelist left without your spiritual father must have been hard on Timothy. Timothy probably dealt with lots of persecution and the hardships that come with being a disciple. Paul tells Timothy that God has given him, and by extension, all Christians, a spirit of power, love, and discipline.

Power, love, and discipline from God triumphs over the fear from inside of you.

If we do not recognize the spirit God has given us all, why “kindle afresh” the gifts God has given us? Why try? What’s the point? But, if we have such a great spirit, you can do what Paul says next…

Paul tells Timothy that because he has such a great spirit from God, he is not to be ashamed of God and Paul’s testimony. The reason we so often are ashamed is because we are not confident. We must be confident in the message of the Gospel.

Instead, Paul tells Timothy to join him in suffering for the gospel. How could Timothy embrace suffering? How could any of us? Because we do so according to God’s power… He saved us! We can suffer because we have been called to God’s purpose.

We may not like suffering, but God has purpose for us.

Paul shares how he himself is suffering: being a preacher, apostle, and teacher. But, Paul is not ashamed. Actually, Paul is confident. Paul has the confidence Timothy lacked. That’s what was keeping Timothy from being an even better evangelist (and disciple). If you want to be an effective believer, you must be confident in 2 things: knowing Jesus and that Jesus is able to keep your soul until Judgment Day.

If you know Jesus, you know the truth.

If Jesus will ultimately save you, then all the suffering is worth it. All the devotion is worth it.

Paul is calling Timothy to be like him: working hard in the Kingdom, yet unashamed. You need to do and be both.

If you want to be useful to the Lord, you cannot be ashamed. Shame hinders good workers. Christians who are ashamed of the gospel (which is essentially the same as being ashamed of Christ) is like a child who is ashamed he or she has parents.

What gifts have we been suppressing because we are scared of persecution or suffering? Don’t be afraid, be confident in the Gospel and Christ. Embrace the suffering, knowing God has purpose for your life. We have a great spirit from God, we can endure.

“For I consider that the sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory that is to be revealed to us” (Romans 8:18).

To God be the glory.
