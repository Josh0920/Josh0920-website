Kindle Afresh — Join With Me in Suffering

Joshua M. Haub · April 8, 2023

Paul's second letter to Timothy is his last. He is in prison, awaiting execution. And one of the first things he addresses is Timothy's apparent timidity — a gift that has been allowed to cool, a spirit that has begun to shrink back.

"For this reason I remind you to kindle afresh the gift of God which is in you through the laying on of my hands. For God has not given us a spirit of timidity, but of power and love and discipline." (2 Timothy 1:6–7)

Kindle afresh. The image is of a fire that has died down to embers — still alive, but not burning the way it should. Paul is not rebuking Timothy for losing his faith. He is urging him to fan what is still there back into flame. The gift is still in him. It needs to be stirred up.

The spirit of timidity — the fear that holds back, that stays quiet when it should speak, that retreats when it should advance — that spirit did not come from God. Power, love, and discipline came from God. Fear did not.

Then Paul makes a direct and personal appeal:

"Therefore do not be ashamed of the testimony of our Lord or of me His prisoner, but join with me in suffering for the gospel according to the power of God." (2 Timothy 1:8)

Join with me in suffering. Paul is not promising Timothy that faithfulness will be easy or safe. He is inviting him into the same cost he is paying. Suffering for the gospel is not an unfortunate side effect — it is the path.

And then Paul anchors his own steadiness in something deeper than circumstances:

"I know whom I have believed and I am convinced that He is able to guard what I have entrusted to Him until that day." (2 Timothy 1:12)

The fire can be rekindled. The fear can be faced. The suffering can be joined. Because the One we have believed is able.

To Him Be the Glory.
