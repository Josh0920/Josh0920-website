Titus 2 — “So That”

Joshua M. Haub · April 4, 2025

In Titus 2, there are 3 “So That”s (v. 5, 8, 10). These “so that”s are what the “result” of following these teachings will cause.

In v. 5, women needed to be subject to their husbands “so that” God’s word would not be dishonored (Blasphemed, βλασφημέω)

In v. 8, young men (specifically Titus), needed to be an example and sound in speech “so that” Titus’ opponents (false teachers, 1:10ff) would have nothing to accuse him (and God’s people (v. 2–8)) of

In v. 10, bondslaves were to submit and obey their masters, showing good faith “so that” others would respect God’s word

What do all these “so that’s” have in common? They point to us being Christ’s representatives. Our conduct matters. If we act Worldly-like, our religion will be totally disregarded. Is that what we want? No! For God to be respected among those of the world, we must show it to be respectable. We cannot say we follow Jesus, but our conduct says otherwise. That is exactly what the false teachers were doing (cf. 1:16). God has called us to be different than the world. We are called by a Holy (set apart) calling. How can we be set apart if we still live Worldly-like? The point in 2:2–10 is that we are supposed to be Christ-like.

Are we being good representatives for Christ, or is our conduct resulting in God’s word being “dishonored” (v. 5), giving our opponents ammunition to disregard God’s word (v. 8), and God’s doctrine being disrespected (v. 10)?

To Him be the Glory.
