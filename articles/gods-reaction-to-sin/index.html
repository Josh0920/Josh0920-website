God’s Reaction to Sin

Joshua M. Haub · November 9, 2024

Zephaniah opens with some of the most severe language in all the prophets. God is speaking, and He does not soften it:

"I will completely remove all things from the face of the earth," declares the Lord. (Zephaniah 1:2)

Everything. Man, animals, birds, fish. A sweeping, total removal. Then He narrows the focus to Judah and Jerusalem specifically — to those who should have known better, who had the Law and the prophets and the temple and still turned away.

And then chapter 1 verse 15 describes the day that is coming:

"A day of wrath is that day, a day of trouble and distress, a day of destruction and desolation, a day of darkness and gloom, a day of clouds and thick darkness." (Zephaniah 1:15)

We live in a world that has largely decided God is tolerant of sin — that He is patient to the point of indifference, that love means acceptance of whatever we choose. Zephaniah will not let us stay there. Neither will any honest reading of Scripture.

No one takes sin more seriously than God does. That is not a peripheral truth. It is the reason Jesus went to the cross. The cross was not God softening His reaction to sin — it was God absorbing His own reaction so that we would not have to.

If we understood what sin costs — what it actually cost — we would not be casual about it in our own lives. We would grieve it. We would fight it. We would flee it.

How seriously do we take it?

To Him Be the Glory.
