Are We Waiting or Working?

Joshua M. Haub · August 12, 2023

Today has been an emotionally challenging day for many. As I am writing this, I have, within the hour, gotten out of a singing in which we sang several hymns written by Matthew W. Bassford in light of his recent passing. Thinking about his passing today has caused me to reflect on the impact he has had on so many. So, I thought I would share it.

A few days ago, I was in New York in which we sang several of his hymns because of his declining health. Those there were informed the next morning, his caretaker had been converted the previous morning. That was amazing.

What amazes me is not just the incredible fact that there is going to be, Lord willing, another soul in Heaven, but the fact that Matt, on his deathbed, was still preaching the Gospel.

How easy it would have been to lay there in bed saying: “I’m just waiting to die,” and become tempted to stop working my hardest for the Lord or for others. That would have been my excuse, perhaps it would have been yours too. I’d like to present a different approach I see from Matt:

Matt posted several times in the last month, still taking time to teach and encourage us when he could have taken a backseat. He literally worked to help convert someone while he was dying. Think about that for a moment.

It makes me wonder what I am doing while I am living in perfectly good health with abilities I could be using better.

I am reminded of the Parable of The Talents from the Gospel of Matthew. God has entrusted us all, while we are here, with an amount of gifts. Matt used his gift of literature to impact so many. He used his suffering, not as a way to promote himself, but as a way to get people to take the Truth seriously. His life touched many. His life touched me. God had given him the gift of writing and the platform to use it, and By God’s Glory, he used it.

Are you using your gifts for God’s glory? God told the good stewards in the parable:

“‘Well done, good and faithful slave. You were faithful with a few things, I will put you in charge of many things; enter into the joy of your master.’” Matthew 25:21, 23

We all have gifts. We all must use them for God’s glory. Matt taught me something:

We are not simply just waiting to get to Heaven; we are working until we get to Heaven.

There is a difference.

Are you willing to work for Him?

It is fitting that, in one of my college classes, we have been talking about Suffering. It has been powerful to me, knowing some of the things my professor is currently struggling with. So, I’d like to quote him:

“If my weakness can help someone else, let’s glorify God with it!”

Consider Job. If you knew your suffering would impact the masses, would you go through it in order to give them hope?

Matt had a weakness, my professor has a weakness, I have a weakness, and you have a weakness. So, my call to you is this: let’s work until we get to Heaven and “let’s glorify God with it!”

“And He has said to me, ‘My grace is sufficient for you, for power is perfected in weakness.’ Most gladly, therefore, I will rather boast about my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may dwell in me. Therefore I am well content with weaknesses, with insults, with distresses, with persecutions, with difficulties, for Christ’s sake; for when I am weak, then I am strong.” 2 Corinthians 12:9–10

To Him be the glory.
