Praying for Famines

Joshua M. Haub · February 7, 2025

The Prodigal Son left home with his inheritance, went to a distant country, and wasted everything. He had money, freedom, and a life built entirely around himself. And then:

"Now when he had spent everything, a severe famine occurred in that country, and he began to be impoverished." (Luke 15:14)

The famine is not incidental to the story. It is the turning point. While the son had resources, he had no reason to go back. The famine stripped away the last layer of cushion between him and reality. And it was only when he was feeding pigs and longing for their food that he came to his senses.

"He came to his senses." He woke up. And he went home.

I think about people I know who are living far from God. People I love who are comfortable in their distance — comfortable enough that there is no felt need to return. And I wonder: do I love them enough to pray for a famine?

Not because I want them to suffer. The father in the parable did not want his son to suffer. But the father knew something the son did not yet know: that coming home was worth whatever it cost to get there.

Sometimes the most loving thing we can pray for is not comfort and ease. Sometimes it is the hard providence that removes the false cushions and leaves a person with nowhere to turn but God.

Do we love people enough to pray that honestly? To ask God to do whatever it takes — even the painful things — to bring them home?

To Him Be the Glory.
