Reflection

Podcast Episode — Job

Joshua M. Haub · March 4, 2023

We released a new episode of What Does It Say? this week — our study of the book of Job. I wanted to share some of what has been on my mind as we work through it.

The book opens in heaven with a conversation that Job never hears. God points to Job as an example of righteousness. Satan's response frames everything that follows:

"Does Job fear God for nothing?" (Job 1:9)

That is the accusation beneath all the suffering. Satan's claim is that no one serves God for His own sake — everyone has a price, a breaking point, a level of loss at which they will stop. Job is only righteous, Satan argues, because righteousness pays. Remove the hedge, take away the blessings, and Job will curse God to His face.

God's answer is to let Satan test that claim. And Job's suffering — the loss of his children, his wealth, his health — is the test.

Job's friends arrive and spend most of the book offering explanations. Their theology is tidy: suffering is punishment, therefore Job must have sinned. They are confident. They are also wrong. God tells them so directly at the end of the book and requires them to offer sacrifices and have Job pray for them.

Job, who argued and questioned and cried out to God throughout — Job is the one vindicated. His honesty before God was more righteous than his friends' neat explanations.

The central question the book raises is not "why do the righteous suffer?" It is deeper than that: are there people in this world willing to fear God for nothing — to be faithful even when it costs everything, even when no explanation is given?

Job was one of them. The book exists as evidence that the answer to Satan's question is yes.

To Him Be the Glory.
