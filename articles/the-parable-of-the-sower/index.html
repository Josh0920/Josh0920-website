The Parable of the Sower

Joshua M. Haub · November 14, 2025

Jesus tells the crowd a parable about a farmer scattering seed. The seed is the same in every case. What differs is the soil.

"Behold, the sower went out to sow; and as he sowed, some seeds fell beside the road, and the birds came and ate them up. Others fell on the rocky places, where they did not have much soil... And others fell on the thorns, and the thorns came up and choked them out. And others fell on the good soil and yielded a crop, some a hundredfold, some sixty, and some thirty." (Matthew 13:3–8)

When the disciples asked why He spoke in parables, Jesus explained that the parables reveal truth to those who are willing to see it — and conceal it from those who are not. Then He gave the interpretation: the seed is the word of the kingdom. The soils are the conditions of the heart when the word arrives.

The hard path — the word is heard but immediately stolen away. There was no entry point.

The rocky soil — the word is received with joy, but there is no root. When trouble or persecution comes because of the word, the person falls away. The initial enthusiasm was real, but it was not deep.

The thorny soil — the word takes root, but the worries of the world and the deceitfulness of riches crowd in and choke it. Something else fills the space the word needs.

The good soil — the word is heard, understood, and bears fruit. Not uniformly — some a hundredfold, some sixty, some thirty — but fruit nonetheless.

I do not think the parable is only about first conversion. I think it describes something that happens daily. Every time the word comes to us — in Scripture, in the assembly, in study — the question is: what kind of soil am I being right now?

Am I hard and distracted? Am I shallow and enthusiastic but unrooted? Am I choked by things I refuse to let go of? Or am I soft, receptive, willing to let the word go all the way down?

It is a daily choice. Not a one-time category you fall into.

To Him Be the Glory.
