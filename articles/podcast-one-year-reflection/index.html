Podcast — One Year Reflection

Joshua M. Haub · January 10, 2025

I started this podcast a little over a year ago. When I started, my objective was to provide people with an additional view as they studied through the book of Job. Sometimes, when I’m studying something, I will cross reference others’ opinions (men who I trust) to see what they have to say about a passage. I wanted this podcast to serve the same purpose. No, it’s not professionally done, but I pray it helps those who need it. It helps me to study the Text and record them. Listen and use them, if you wish.

Lord willing, after Job, I’d like to continue working through other books. I believe the next will be Philippians, but that could change between now and when Job is completed.

SYNOPSIS: Job is a book that pushes us to see that those who remain faithful and righteous through suffering will be blessed by God (either in this life or the next) and our sufferings can help others’ through their own. There are people who are willing to suffer for being righteous, even if there seems to be no benefit in this life.

“Nevertheless the righteous will hold to his way,
And he who has clean hands will grow stronger and stronger” (Job 17:9, NASB95).
“As for me, I know that my Redeemer lives,
And at the last He will take His stand on the earth” (Job 19:25, NASB95).

To Him be the Glory.
