Podcast — One Year Reflection

Joshua M. Haub · January 10, 2025

The What Does It Say? podcast has been going for one year now, and we have spent most of that year in Job. It has been one of the most rewarding stretches of study I can remember.

The book of Job is deceptively simple on the surface — a righteous man suffers, his friends offer explanations, God speaks from the whirlwind. But underneath all of that is a question that cuts to the heart of everything:

"Does Job fear God for nothing?" (Job 1:9)

That is Satan's accusation. The implication: Job is only righteous because it pays. God has put a hedge around him, blessed his work, made him prosperous. Remove all of that, Satan says, and Job will curse God to His face.

In other words: no one serves God for His own sake. Everyone has a price.

Job's suffering is God's answer to that accusation. And Job's persistence through it — his refusal to curse God even in the depths of his grief and confusion — is the proof that Satan was wrong.

Job's friends, for all their theology, were wrong about him. They assumed suffering meant punishment. God corrected them directly. Job, who had complained loudly and honestly, was the one vindicated.

After a year in this book, I keep coming back to the central question: are there people in this world willing to be righteous even if it means suffering? Even if faithfulness costs everything?

Are you one of them?

To Him Be the Glory.
