Time Time Time

Joshua M. Haub · October 21, 2023

Time is equally as inescapable as it is limited. We are all limited by time. There is only a certain amount we all have. How do we use it?

“Therefore be careful how you walk, not as unwise men but as wise, making the most of your time, because the days are evil.” (Ephesians 5:15–16, NASB1995)

The apostle Paul calls for us to use the time we have well. How are you using your time? You may feel like, “I don’t have enough time in a day!” This thinking tends to lead us to make excuses for why we manage our time poorly, though we may have legitimate reasons for why we make this statement.

There is only so many hours in a day, days in a week, weeks in a year, years in a lifetime. The amount of days each one has is unknown, but God knows how long each of us have. You may live 50 more years, 30 more, 10 more, 1 more, or less. You may die tomorrow, or tonight. You do not know. The question, then, is not “Why do I feel like I don’t have enough time?” Or even the statement, “I wish I had more!” But, instead, “What am I doing with the time I do have?”

A friend of mine who used to play for the same basketball organization as me is currently in extreme critical condition. He was in a car accident yesterday. His name is Kole, please pray for him. Last I heard, he is still fighting to stay alive. Just the other day, he was at a sporting event with his family. Consider these things and keep him and his family in your prayers.

The time left in your life is always ticking. Always. The end is inescapable. No amount of human effort will keep you from facing your end. The only hope we have for “escaping” death is the coming return of Christ, but even then, your “life” here on earth will still “end.” Your life is ticking away. Even as I write this and revise it, my life is only getting shorter. So, how will I… how will you… use the time we have left?

In this stage of my life, in college, time has become almost obsolete. Time is one big blur. My days run together. Things that happened last week feel like a month ago. Things that happened a month ago feel like a year ago, or longer. I could easily make the excuse, “I’m too busy for x.y.z.” Admittedly, I have made that excuse. In some things, I still am. When will I decide to change my language? Instead of the statement: “I’m too busy with …,” when will I begin to ask the question: “Why am I not more busy for the Lord?” When will you too?

How much time are we wasting? Having fun is nice, but is life not more than entertainment and laughter? If you looked at your life, do you have more Screen Time on your phone than you do Bible Time in the Word?

We all make excuses. But, Paul’s statement still rings true: “The days are evil” (Eph 5:16b). In one sense, time is working against you. You only have so much. The question is, how will you use it for the Lord?

Time is ticking; use it well.

You just never know…

To Him be the glory.

Joshua M. Haub · October 21, 2023
