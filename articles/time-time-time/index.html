Time, Time, Time

Joshua M. Haub · October 21, 2023

Paul gives a command that cuts right through one of our most common excuses:

"Therefore be careful how you walk, not as unwise men but as wise, making the most of your time, because the days are evil." (Ephesians 5:15–16)

Making the most of your time. The Greek word behind "making the most" — exagorazomenoi — carries the idea of buying up, redeeming, seizing what is available before the opportunity closes. Time is the resource. The days are evil, which means they are not neutral ground. They are working against you. If you are not actively redeeming the time, something else is consuming it.

We say we are too busy. But busy doing what? Busy is not the same as fruitful. We have more screen time than Bible time. More hours given to entertainment than to prayer, to serving, to being with our families in any meaningful way. We are not too busy — we are making choices, and we are choosing the lesser thing.

Time is the one resource that cannot be recovered. Money lost can be regained. Health can sometimes be restored. But an hour spent is gone. A year passed does not come back. The opportunity to speak to that person, to be present with your child, to study, to serve — it closes.

Stop making excuses about being too busy. Look honestly at where the hours actually go. Then decide whether you are walking wisely or not.

The days are evil. Redeem the time.

To Him Be the Glory.
