Reflection

Philippians 2

Joshua M. Haub · September 14, 2024

Chapter 2:

Paul continues on in chapter 1 by speaking about conducting yourself “in a manner worthy of the Gospel” (1:27). Why? Well, he explains that our unity helps us stand against false teachers, which he circles back around and speaks on in chapter 3. From 1:27 on, Paul is building the basis for 1:27–28. We need unity and here are our examples (ch. 1:27–2:30). Why unity? Because of false teaching (ch. 3:1–3), and here is the attitude and mindset we need (ch. 3:4–21). Finally, Paul ends the letter by telling the Philippians: “You have problems with unity. Euodia and Syntyche, STOP IT.” Paul not only circles back around to the topic of unity, but he gives practical advice (4:4–9), and concludes by speaking about the most unifying thing the Philippians love doing: Sending money (4:10–19).

Well, Paul builds his argument in chapter 2. Unity (ch. 2) is needed to defeat false teaching (ch. 3). Here’s how one does it.

I will list these things out (2:2–4):

  • Being of the same mind
  • Maintaining the same love
  • United in spirit
  • Intent on one purpose
  • Doing nothing from selfishness or empty conceit
  • Humble-minded in regarding others as more important than ourselves
  • Not looking at what we want, but what others want

Did you notice, Paul switches from unity to selflessness. Why? Because without selflessness, there is no unity. A church full of “it’s my way or the highway” people will never be unified. If everyone wants worship at different times, then a final decision will never be made. Selflessness is the foundation of unity.

“The true obstacle to unity is not the presence of legitimate differences of opinion but self-centeredness” (Silva, Moisés. Philippians. 2nd ed., Baker Academic, 2005, p. 87.)

Then, Paul goes on to speak about the Mind of Christ. Here it is in a nutshell: Jesus (God’s equal) gave up everything: From Heaven to earth. But, He did not stop there. He went from Heaven to earth to servant to death. If God is willing to serve us by dying, how much more should we serve one another? Are we really better than God? Of course not.

Service has nothing to do with weakness or insignificance, but everything to do with love.

Paul lists 2 other men that the Philippians can practically emulate: Timothy and Epaproditus. Both men of Christ-mindedness. Timothy: “genuinely… concerned for your welfare… proven worth…” (2:20, 22). Epaphroditus: “…he came close to death for the work of Christ, risking his life to complete what was deficient in your service to me” (v. 30). These men showed the Philippians what the Mind of Christ was all about: Others and Self-sacrifice.

And, as Paul puts it, we must be careful because “…[we] appear as lights in the world” (v. 15). The world is watching us.

How are we going to be united in our congregations? By being selfless, humble, putting others first, caring for others, and self-sacrifice. Are we really better than God? Of course not.

To Him be the Glory.
