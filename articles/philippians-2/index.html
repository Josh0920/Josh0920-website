Reflection

Philippians 2

Joshua M. Haub · September 14, 2024

Philippians 2 is one of the richest chapters in the New Testament. It begins with a call to unity — and it grounds that unity in something specific:

"Do nothing from selfishness or empty conceit, but with humility of mind regard one another as more important than yourselves; do not merely look out for your own personal interests, but also for the interests of others." (Philippians 2:3–4)

The path to unity runs through selflessness. We cannot be unified while we are competing. We cannot be one while we are each trying to be first.

And then Paul points to the supreme example:

"Have this attitude in yourselves which was also in Christ Jesus, who, although He existed in the form of God, did not regard equality with God a thing to be grasped, but emptied Himself, taking the form of a bond-servant, and being made in the likeness of men. Being found in appearance as a man, He humbled Himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross." (Philippians 2:5–8)

Heaven to earth. Glory to flesh. Master to servant. Life to death — and not just death, but the most shameful death imaginable. That is the descent of Christ. And Paul says: have this mind in you.

Then, at the end of the chapter, Paul gives two living examples of this same pattern. Timothy, who genuinely cares for others rather than his own interests. Epaphroditus, who came close to death serving the work of the gospel and risking his life to make up for what was lacking in the Philippians' service to Paul.

The mind of Christ is not an abstract ideal. It shows up in real people making real choices. People who go, who serve, who risk. Who put others first — not because it is easy, but because that is what Jesus did.

To Him Be the Glory.
