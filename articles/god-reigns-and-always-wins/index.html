Reflection

God Reigns and Always Wins

Joshua M. Haub · April 12, 2024

As of late, I’ve been spending a lot of time in the books of Daniel and Revelation because I’ve been apart of classes (and personal study) of these 2 books. I’ve also heard sermons preached this year on the 7 churches of Asia. What better time to be reading and studying these 2 books than within an Election Year here in America! I’ve never really studied Apocalyptic Literature like this before (deep, practical, insightful studying). It honestly has been super helpful. Who knew Daniel 7–12 and Revelation would become apart of my “favorite” books of the Bible list! I want us to examine the book of Daniel, Revelation, and Ourselves.

1. The book of Daniel:

In chapters 7–12, Daniel experienced 3 visions (ch. 7, 8, 10–12) in which God (through angels and imagery) showed Daniel that the coming kingdoms were going to rise and fall, wage wars, and have conflicts (internal and external). God’s people would even suffer. Yet, in the end, God would win! These visions were meant to help the Jews in the period between the Old and New Testaments endure through the trials that came with the time period of these kingdoms (especially under the rule of Antiochus Epiphanies, ch. 11:21–12:13). The Jews needed to know God was going to win because of the severe persecution they would face under kings like Antiochus Epiphanies. Daniel’s book ends:

“‘How blessed is he who keeps waiting and attains to the 1,335 days!’” (12:12, NASB1995)

The angel told Daniel that the Jews who endured and remained faithful until the end of the trials of the Intertestinomal period would be blessed.

The Jews needed such a message! It offered encouragement and reason to continue on even in the face of backlash.

2. The book of Revelation:

The book of Revelation has a similar message to Christians living during the Roman time period. There would be opposition from both Jews (Synagogue of Satan, 2:9 / 3:9) and the Roman Government. Ultimately, Satan was behind the Roman persecution of the Christians in the first century. Yet, the story ends the same as Daniel: “Endure the suffering!” Christ is coming quickly and He has His reward with Him (22:12). Christ will reward everyone for their own individual actions. So, act righteously! Even in the midst of persecution, be righteous and endure it, because He is coming quickly!

The Christians needed this reminder in the 1st century because of the severe persecution they would endure by the hands of the government and Jews.

3. And us:

Do we need these lessons today? Have we ever been persecuted? Perhaps not like the Jews (Daniel) or Christians (Revelation), but could we? What if one day, we must endure the same kind of persecution? What about when others turn on us? We must endure just like the Jews and Christians did! Part of being a Christian is learning to glorify God in suffering for Him. Christianity is not a “feel-good-about-yourself” kind of religion! It is a steadfast commitment to serve Him even in the harshest of circumstances.

We must remember that in the end, GOD WINS. And those who endure will receive the reward of eternal life! We cannot give up.

Why is this important in an Election year? Because no matter the outcome of who is elected, nor the effect of their laws (e.g. laws that give way to sin or persecution of Christ’s Church), God is still in control (Romans 13:1, Daniel 3:17) and His kingdom is the ONLY kingdom that will endure forever (Daniel 3:3, 34).

America is not God’s Nation, nor His Kingdom. Heaven is God’s Kingdom and His people are His Nation. We must, like Daniel, learn to remain faithful to Him no matter who is in power, whether we agree with their policies or not. Our citizenship is in Heaven (Phil 3:20). Let us not fight for rights, but fight for souls. Let us fight and fulfill our calling: Save Souls. That is the Kingdom Call (Matt 28:18–20). Let us, in all situations, learn to follow Christ while submitting to the government authorities, and remember that God is in control.

The message of Apocalyptic Literature: Endure, knowing the outcome is secure: God wins, the wicked are punished, and the righteous will be blessed.

To Him be the Glory.
