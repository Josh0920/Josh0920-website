Reflection

God Reigns and Always Wins

Joshua M. Haub · April 12, 2024

Daniel 7 through 12 is a series of visions of earthly kingdoms — beasts rising from the sea, empires in conflict, kings who exalt themselves to the heavens. It is dizzying and strange. But it all points to one thing: every kingdom that rises will fall, and the kingdom of God will stand forever.

The Ancient of Days takes His seat. The court sits in judgment. The beast is slain. And one like a Son of Man comes with the clouds of heaven and is given dominion and glory and a kingdom that will not be destroyed.

Revelation echoes the same message. Babylon falls. The beast is defeated. The Lamb wins. The kingdoms of this world become the kingdom of our Lord and of His Christ.

This is not abstract prophecy. It is a word for people living under pressure — people who look at the political landscape and feel afraid, who see powerful forces arrayed against God's people and wonder whether God is paying attention.

He is paying attention. He has always been paying attention. Every empire that has ever tried to stand against Him is gone. Every king who exalted himself above God has been brought low. This is the pattern. It does not change.

America is not God's nation. No nation is. God's nation is the church — people from every tribe, tongue, and language, gathered under the lordship of Christ. Our citizenship is in heaven.

Stop fighting for political power. Fight for souls. That is the war that actually matters, and it is the one God has already won.

To Him Be the Glory.

Joshua M. Haub · April 12, 2024
