Do I Really Have Faith?

Joshua M. Haub · June 10, 2023

“Do I really have faith?”

Several days ago, I had a conversation with a dear friend of mine. We sat and talked about various topics. This conversation happened later in the evening (the best time for deep talks!). One of the topics we came to was (to keep from being too specific) the idea of the underlying issues that plague us as Christians.

We discussed the things we see in Christians that sadden us. Things that hurt us. Things we know need to be worked on. Some of these things we even saw in ourselves.

We came to a point in our conversation where we began to identify 2 of the underlying issues of why Christians, ourselves included, are falling short. These 2 ideas are connected, yet different.

#1 — “Do I have faith?”

Faith is the anchor for everything we do in this life. It is the basis for our walking with Christ. If we do not believe, we will not obey. If we will not obey, we will not be “Christian.” If we are not “Christian,” we are no different than the world. In fact, we are the world. Faith is the foundation. It is the basis for which we trust God.

#2 — “Do I really have faith?”

This question, to me, lies even deeper than the first. The first is answered by all Christians fairly easily: “Yes, of course I have faith!” However, this question asks us to look even deeper than our surface-level intellect. In fact, this question begs us to open our hearts to the very core of our existence. We must ask ourselves these questions: If I do have faith, why then do I sin? If I do have faith, why do I lie? If I do have faith, why do I stress? If I do have faith, why do I worry? If I do have faith, why do I trust in myself? If I do have faith, why do I think I’m better than others? If I do have faith, why do I put myself before others and God? If I do have faith, why do I doubt?

Another way of asking this question: “Do I really trust God?” And if I do really trust God, why do I take try to control my life when instead I need to allow God to be God?

The sad reality we may need to face is, “Perhaps I don’t have the deepest kind of faith I ought to.” We all must be honest with ourselves. Hiding from reality only prolongs the inevitable pain. I don’t have the deepest kind of faith I need; perhaps you identify with me. External problems can be a result of a deeper, internal issue. You see, faith is the core. The deep core. The bare bones of a Christian’s existence. If my faith is not really in God, it will be in something else. If that is the case, we will never become what God wants us to be. Our Walk with Christ will only be half-hearted, even superficial at times. That ‘thing’ my faith is in will become my God. But, if YHWH is my God, I have everything I will need in this life. If He is my God, then I will serve Him with my entire heart, not just the convenient parts. My spirituality will grow while my love for this world will shrink. If I really have faith, I can become the fullest measure of Christ I could possibly attain.

So, ask yourself these 2 questions:

Do I have faith?

But deeper,

Do I really have faith? … Do I really trust God?

To Him be the glory.
