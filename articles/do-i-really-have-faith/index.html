Do I Really Have Faith?

Joshua M. Haub · June 10, 2023

Two conversations I keep having — sometimes with others, sometimes with myself.

The first: "Do I have faith?" That question usually points to Hebrews 11:1:

"Now faith is the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen." (Hebrews 11:1)

Assurance. Conviction. Not a vague feeling of religious interest, but a settled confidence in what God has said about things we cannot yet see. Do you have that?

The second conversation goes deeper: "Do I really trust God?" And that is where it gets harder. Because there is a kind of belief that stops short of trust.

James makes a jarring observation:

"You believe that God is one. You do well; the demons also believe, and shudder." (James 2:19)

Demons believe. They know exactly who God is — they have no doubts about His existence or His power. Their belief is accurate. And it does nothing for them, because it is not trust. It is knowledge without surrender.

The question, then, is not only whether you believe certain things about God. It is whether you actually trust Him — whether you are resting your life on Him, whether your choices and your peace and your priorities flow from genuine reliance on who He is.

If your faith is not truly in God, it is in something else. And whatever that something else is — your own ability, your finances, your relationships, your health — that thing has become your functional god. You will protect it above all others. You will be most afraid when it is threatened.

What are you actually trusting in?

To Him Be the Glory.
