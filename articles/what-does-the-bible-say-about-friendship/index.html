Make Good Friends and Be One Too

Joshua M. Haub · May 22, 2026

Solomon had a lot to say about friendship. And what he said was not flattering toward the way most of us approach it.

We tend to collect acquaintances. We accumulate followers, connections, contacts. But Proverbs is after something different — a friend who is actually there when it matters.

"A friend loves at all times, and a brother is born for a time of adversity." (Proverbs 17:17)

Not a fair-weather friend. Not someone who is around when things are good and disappears when they are hard. A friend who loves at all times. That is a high standard. And it is the standard God sets.

Solomon also says:

"Faithful are the wounds of a friend, but deceitful are the kisses of an enemy." (Proverbs 27:6)

A real friend will tell you the truth. Not to be cruel, but because they actually care what happens to you. The person who flatters you, who never challenges you, who only tells you what you want to hear — that person is not a friend. They are doing you harm.

And then there is this:

"Iron sharpens iron, so one man sharpens another." (Proverbs 27:17)

Iron sharpening iron is not gentle. There is friction. There is resistance. But the result is that both pieces become sharper. That is what true friendship does — it makes you better, even when it is uncomfortable.

Here is what I keep coming back to: we spend so much time wanting good friends, looking for good friends, wishing we had better friends — and not nearly enough time asking whether we are being a good friend. Solomon describes what a real friend looks like. We should read that description and ask whether we fit it.

Do you love at all times? Do you tell the truth when it would be easier to say nothing? Do you stay when things get hard?

Make good friends. And be one too.

To Him Be the Glory.
