The Message of 2 Dying Men

Joshua M. Haub · July 22, 2023

Earlier today, I had a study with a dear friend of mine in which we discussed part of John 12. In this study, we noted a couple of times the fact that Jesus was nearing the end of His life (cf. 12:1, 12:12). Jesus was heavily focused on the Coming Trial (cf. 12:23, 27). Jesus knew that He was going into Jerusalem for the last time; He wasn’t going to be leaving this city alive.

What would that have been like? If you knew you would die, what would you do? What would you say to those you love?

Would you preach the gospel? Repent from past sins? Make those broken relationships right?

I know what you would not do: waste time.

Jesus focused heavily on others (ch 12–17) and worked to prepare the disciples for His death (especially ch 13–17).

Jesus was a dying man. He knew His time to die had come (12:23). Yet, what did He teach? Here is one thing Jesus said:

“He who loves his life loses it, and he who hates his life in this world will keep it to life eternal” (12:25, NASB1995).

The person who is so fixated on this life… getting famous, wealth, status, sports, vacations, perfect family life, etc… the person who has become too busy for the Lord because they are so focused on “making it here…” that person is going to LOSE their eternal life.

The person who gives up trying to “make it big now” and is fixated on preparing for the Next Life will serve God and find Eternal Life in the end.

We’ve become too fixated on this life when we really need to be preparing for the Next One.

Jesus was a dying man with a message that ought to ring even stronger in our ears in light of that fact.

No coincidence that a few decades later, Peter wrote 2 Peter. I like to call 2 Peter: “Reminders Before I Go.” This truly is “The Letter of A Dying Man.”

2 Peter 1:13–14: “I consider it right, as long as I am in this earthly dwelling, to stir you up by way of reminder, knowing that the laying aside of my earthly dwelling is imminent, as also our Lord Jesus Christ has made clear to me.”

Peter wrote this letter knowing he would die soon, thus he wrote specific reminders he wanted the brethren to remember. In chapter 3, Peter wrote:

“But the day of the Lord will come like a thief, in which the heavens will pass away with a roar and the elements will be destroyed with intense heat, and the earth and its works will be burned up. Since all these things are to be destroyed in this way, what sort of people ought you to be in holy conduct and godliness, looking for and hastening the coming day of God…” (3:10–12a)

Can you see how what Jesus said is the same as what Peter said? Both are dying men with the same message: This world isn’t going to last… if you are going to try and make it here, you are going to be sorely disappointed in The End.

If we want to live as God’s people, forget storing up treasures here, store them up in Eternity.

To Him be the glory.
