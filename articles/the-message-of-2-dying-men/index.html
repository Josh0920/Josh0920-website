The Message of 2 Dying Men

Joshua M. Haub · July 22, 2023

Two men, separated by decades, both knowing they were about to die. Both left us a message. And remarkably, they said the same thing.

Jesus, in John 12, knew the cross was days away. He had entered Jerusalem, Greeks were coming to see Him, and He said to His disciples:

"He who loves his life loses it, and he who hates his life in this world will keep it to life eternal." (John 12:25)

Do not cling to this world. The one who holds on to his life here will lose the life that matters. The one who releases it — who treats this present life as secondary — keeps it into eternity.

Peter, near the end of his life, knowing his death was coming (2 Peter 1:14), wrote his second letter. In chapter 3 he describes what is coming for this world:

"But the day of the Lord will come like a thief, in which the heavens will pass away with a roar and the elements will be destroyed with intense heat, and the earth and its works will be burned up." (2 Peter 3:10)

Everything visible — the heavens, the elements, the earth, the works built on it — will be destroyed. Then Peter draws the conclusion:

"Since all these things are to be destroyed in this way, what sort of people ought you to be in holy conduct and godliness?" (2 Peter 3:11)

Two dying men. One message: this world is not your home and it will not last. Stop building your life around it. Store up what is eternal.

If you knew you were dying, would it change how you live? Live that way now.

To Him Be the Glory.
