For Me to Live Is Christ

Joshua M. Haub · July 6, 2024

Philippians 1:21 is one of the most quoted verses in the New Testament. But the second half of it only makes sense if the first half is true:

"For to me, to live is Christ and to die is gain." (Philippians 1:21)

To die is gain — but only for the person whose life is Christ. If your life is your career, death is loss. If your life is your family, death is loss. If your life is your comfort, your reputation, your plans — death takes all of it.

But if your life is Christ, then death is simply more of what you already have. It is the removal of every limitation and every distraction, and what remains is Him.

Galatians 2:20 is Paul's explanation of how he got there:

"I have been crucified with Christ; and it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me; and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave Himself up for me." (Galatians 2:20)

The old self — the one organized around its own desires, its own identity, its own agenda — has been crucified. It died with Christ. What lives now is not independent of Him. It lives by faith in Him, moment by moment.

So the question is honest and searching: what are we actually living for? When we look at how we spend our time, our attention, our energy, our love — what does the living point toward?

You cannot say "to die is gain" if living is something else.

To Him Be the Glory.
