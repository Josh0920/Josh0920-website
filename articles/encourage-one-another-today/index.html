Encourage One Another “Today”

Joshua M. Haub · May 15, 2026

The writer of Hebrews gives us a very specific command — and a very specific timeframe for it:

"But encourage one another day after day, as long as it is still called 'Today,' so that none of you will be hardened by the deceitfulness of sin." (Hebrews 3:13)

Day after day. As long as it is still called "Today."

That word — Today — is doing a lot of work in this passage. The writer has been quoting Psalm 95, where God warns Israel: "Today, if you hear His voice, do not harden your hearts." The warning is urgent because Today will not always be available. There is a window. And the assumption the writer makes is that sin is actively working to close it.

Sin hardens. That is not a metaphor — it is a description of what happens spiritually when we keep choosing against God. Each time we say no to Him and yes to ourselves, our hearts become a little less sensitive, a little more resistant, a little more closed. It is a slow process. It rarely feels dramatic. But it is happening.

And the antidote, according to Hebrews, is not primarily personal discipline. It is each other.

"Encourage one another day after day." The assumption is that we are in relationship — real relationship, the kind where we know what is happening in each other's lives. You cannot encourage someone you never talk to. You cannot notice hardening in someone you only see on Sunday.

This is a daily call. Not weekly. Not when it feels appropriate. Day after day, for as long as it is still called Today.

Today is still Today. Who do you need to encourage?

To Him Be the Glory.
