Answering Confusing Scenarios

Joshua M. Haub · February 23, 2024

The Sadducees did not believe in the resurrection. So they came to Jesus with a scenario designed to make the resurrection look absurd:

Seven brothers. One woman. Each brother dies and the next marries her, per the levirate law. In the resurrection, whose wife will she be?

It sounds like a genuine puzzle. It was not. It was a trap. The Sadducees were not seeking truth — they were trying to embarrass Jesus by making one of His core teachings look logically incoherent.

Jesus did not take the bait on the surface question. He addressed what was underneath it:

"Is this not the reason you are mistaken, that you do not understand the Scriptures or the power of God?" (Mark 12:24)

Two errors. They did not know the Scriptures — they had not read them carefully enough to know what resurrection actually entails. And they did not know the power of God — they were applying earthly categories to a reality that transcends them. Marriage as we know it is not a feature of the resurrection age. The question assumes a framework that does not hold.

This is a pattern worth learning. When someone brings a confusing scenario — about faith, about ethics, about apparent contradictions in the Bible — the confusion usually lives at a deeper level than the surface question. They are often missing something about what Scripture actually says, or assuming a constraint that does not apply.

The right response is not to scramble for an answer to the exact question as asked. It is to find the underlying issue and address that. That is what Jesus did.

To Him Be the Glory.
