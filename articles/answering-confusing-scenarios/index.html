Answering Confusing Scenarios

Joshua M. Haub · February 23, 2024

Today in a Bible study, I was speaking with one of my friends who brought up a question that I have heard several times before regarding Baptism. Before I pose the question, I will comment that I believe he asked it from a sincere heart.

The question: “What happens to the person who accepts Jesus into their heart, but dies on the way to being baptized?”

You, like me, have probably encountered this question before. The question is usually posed to counteract the idea that a physical baptism is necessary for salvation.

This article is not designed to answer that question specifically, but to answer questions that are framed LIKE this one.

This article is also not designed to deal with situations in which people are seeking honest answers. This article is focused on dealing with questions asked from a hard-heart.

Many times, people who do not like certain Biblical ideas, try to forge scenarios where such ideas seem to “not work.”

The Sadducees did such a thing with Jesus:

“Some Sadducees (who say that there is no resurrection) came to Jesus, and began questioning Him, saying, ‘Teacher, Moses wrote for us that if a man’s brother dies and leaves behind a wife and leaves no child, his brother should marry the wife and raise up children to his brother. There were seven brothers; and the first took a wife, and died leaving no children. The second one married her, and died leaving behind no children; and the third likewise; and so all seven left no children. Last of all the woman died also. In the resurrection, when they rise again, which one’s wife will she be? For all seven had married her’” (Mark 12:18–23, NASB1995).

The Sadducees did not believe in the Biblical idea of “resurrection.” So, they decided to see if they could question Jesus on the matter. They were trying to get Jesus to mess up so they could accuse Him. So, what do you do when you want to trip up someone? You forge a scenario.

Jesus, however, answered this scenario with ease:

“Jesus said to them, ‘Is this not the reason you are mistaken, that you do not understand the Scriptures or the power of God? For when they rise from the dead, they neither marry nor are given in marriage, but are like angels in heaven. But regarding the fact that the dead rise again, have you not read in the book of Moses, in the passage about the burning bush, how God spoke to him, saying, “I am the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob”? He is not the God of the dead, but of the living; you are greatly mistaken’” (Mark 12:24–27).

Jesus answers their question in verse 25, but that was not His main focus. Jesus’ main focus was correcting their faulty interpretation.

The Sadducees misunderstood the Biblical principle of the resurrection, which is what Jesus took the time to correct. Jesus saw the deeper issue with their question and sought to answer it.

I think we fall into the same trap the Sadducees were trying to trap Jesus in. Many times, people are testing us with “outlandish scenarios,” when in reality that is not the real issue at hand.

Take the scenario I posed in the beginning of this article. The issue is not whether the child was saved before baptism or not. The issue is that we try to use scenarios like that to validate our own faulty ideologies.

We must take God at His word. If He says ___, then that is what we believe on that issue. When the Bible doesn’t speak on an issue, we give the best Biblical answer we can find and leave it at that.

When we focus on the scenario, we miss the deeper issue. Jesus saw the deeper issue and addressed that. We must do the same.

When answering, or asking, questions, where are our hearts?

To Him be the glory.
