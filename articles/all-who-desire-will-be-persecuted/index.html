All Who Desire Will Be Persecuted

Joshua M. Haub · April 22, 2023

Paul makes a statement in 2 Timothy 3 that leaves no room for qualification:

"Indeed, all who desire to live godly in Christ Jesus will be persecuted." (2 Timothy 3:12)

Not "may be persecuted." Not "will likely face some difficulty." Will be persecuted. The Greek is a future indicative — a statement of certainty about what is coming. Paul is not issuing a warning about a possibility. He is describing an inevitability.

The logic is simple: godly living in Christ will always create friction with a world that is not. Light does not coexist peacefully with darkness. Salt stings a wound. The world hated Jesus, and He told His disciples plainly that it would hate them too for the same reason.

So here is the question that demands honest reflection: if you are experiencing no friction with the world — if your faith creates no tension, no pushback, no cost — what does that tell you?

There are two possible explanations. Either the world around you has become dramatically more godly. Or you have become more worldly than you realize — accommodating, softening, blending in so well that there is nothing left to push back against.

This is not a call to manufacture conflict or to be needlessly abrasive. It is a call to honest self-examination. The Christian life, lived faithfully, will always cost something. The question is whether we are paying that cost or quietly finding ways around it.

All who desire to live godly will be persecuted. Are you living in a way that makes that promise relevant to you?

To Him Be the Glory.
