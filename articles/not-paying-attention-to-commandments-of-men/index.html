Not Paying Attention to Commandments of Men

Joshua M. Haub · May 2, 2025

“[Titus, teach these false teachers to] not pay attention to Jewish myths and commandments of men who turn away from the truth” (Titus 1:14)

When we read a passage like Titus 1:14, we tend to see the dangers of the false teaching that was happening in Titus’ day as a “past issue.” After all, have you ever had to work with someone on their tendency to believe in “Jewish myths”? However, Paul says something that I believe we should take special note of:

“[do] not pay attention to… commandments of men…” (Titus 1:14).

Paul tells Titus to teach others to fall into the trap of listening to the teachings of people instead of listening to the teachings of God. The false teachers in Titus’ day were saying, “Listen to so and so! They say…” What Titus needed to do was go back and say, “What does GOD say.” Titus needed to help people “be sound in the faith” (1:13).

Do we still struggle with this today? I think so. Maybe we should change “men” to something else…

“[do] not pay attention to… commandments of [preachers]…” (Titus 1:14).

That hits a little closer to home.

“[do] not pay attention to… commandments of [your friends]…” (Titus 1:14).

What we need to do instead is “be sound in the faith” (1:13). Instead of just listening to people because “They say…” We need to ask, “What does GOD say.” After all, some men are “turn[ing] away from the faith” (1:14).

We need to listen to good teachers like our friends and evangelists, but too often we become like the Corinthians: “I am of…” instead of being “of Christ” and His teachings. We follow Christ, not people. “What does GOD say” is more important than “what does my preacher say.”

To Him be the Glory.
