Not Paying Attention to Commandments of Men

Joshua M. Haub · May 2, 2025

Paul writes to Titus about the qualities needed in an elder, and along the way he describes what such a man must stand against:

"Not paying attention to Jewish myths and commandments of men who turn away from the truth." (Titus 1:14)

Commandments of men who turn away from the truth. That is a specific danger — not just wrong teaching, but human instruction dressed up as divine requirement. Rules and expectations that did not come from God but that carry the weight of God's commands in the culture of a congregation or tradition.

The first question we should be asking about any practice, any expectation, any "this is how it must be done" is not "what does my preacher say?" or "what does my tradition say?" but "what does God say?" What does the text actually say?

The Bereans were called noble-minded precisely because they did not simply receive Paul's word. They examined the Scriptures daily to verify it. If anyone ever deserved to be taken at his word it was an apostle — and yet the Bereans checked. That is the standard.

Leaders exist to direct us to Scripture, not to replace it. A good elder, a good preacher, a good teacher — their goal is always to get you into the Word for yourself, not to create dependence on their interpretation. The moment a human voice starts functioning as the final authority rather than a pointer to the final authority, something has gone wrong.

Ask what God says. Then go find out.

To Him Be the Glory.
