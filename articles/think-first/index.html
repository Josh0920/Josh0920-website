Reflection

Think First

Joshua M. Haub · May 10, 2024

How often do I need this reminder:

THINK BEFORE YOU SPEAK

I’ve heard that saying many times in my life, yet it is so true. Solomon wrote:

28 The heart of the righteous ponders how to answer,
but the mouth of the wicked pours out evil things. (Proverbs 15:28, ESV)

If I wish to be wise, I must ponder “How” to answer. “How” suggests more than just “What” to say, but in what tone, attitude, timing, etc. “How” is as important as “What.”

Yet, the wicked “pours” out evil. The word “pours” here means “flow, spring, bubble up, gush, belch out,” etc.

While the Righteous wait, consider, and then answer, the Wicked pour out whatever comes to their mind first.

Speaking like the wicked has never helped me… has it ever helped you? How many times have us thought or said, “I wish I would not have said that.” You can never take back your words nor their damage. James was right, the tongue is like wildfire (James 3). We must be careful with how we use it.

We must learn the Proverb.

Interestingly enough (perhaps purposeful), Solomon writes the following Proverb:

29 The Lord is far from the wicked,
but he hears the prayer of the righteous. (Proverbs 15:28, ESV)

We must be careful that we are not pouring out answers like a wicked person, because wicked people are not close to the Lord. If we wish for our prayers to be heard, we must be wise. Become like the wise person, and listen when your parents tell you:

THINK BEFORE YOU SPEAK

To Him be the Glory.
