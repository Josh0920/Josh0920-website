Reflection

Think First

Joshua M. Haub · May 10, 2024

Solomon draws a sharp contrast in a single verse:

"The heart of the righteous ponders how to answer, but the mouth of the wicked pours out evil things." (Proverbs 15:28)

Two different people. Two different relationships with their words. The righteous one pauses — considers — thinks about what the answer should be before giving it. The wicked one just pours. Whatever is there comes out. No filter. No thought. Just output.

The contrast is not between smart and stupid, or educated and ignorant. It is between righteous and wicked. Solomon is making a moral point. How we handle our words before we say them is a matter of character, not just communication style.

James says something similar with more intensity:

The tongue is a fire. A world of iniquity. It defiles the whole body, sets on fire the course of life, and is itself set on fire by hell. No one can tame it. It is a restless evil, full of deadly poison.

That is not a gentle warning. James is alarmed. He wants us to be alarmed. The tongue — the words we say, the things we type, the responses we fire off before thinking — can do devastating damage. It can destroy relationships, end reputations, wound deeply, and spread like fire through a dry field.

The righteous person ponders. They think about what they are about to say. They consider the effect. They slow down.

Be quick to hear. Slow to speak. Think first.

To Him Be the Glory.
