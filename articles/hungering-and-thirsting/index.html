Hungering and Thirsting

Joshua M. Haub · June 7, 2024

Proverbs 30 contains one of the more unusual observations in the wisdom literature. Agur describes the leech and its two daughters who cry out "Give, Give" — and then lists four things that are never satisfied:

"Sheol, and the barren womb, earth that is never satisfied with water, and fire that never says, 'Enough.'" (Proverbs 30:16)

Four things that just keep consuming. The grave takes and takes and is never full. The barren womb longs and longs. The dry ground drinks and drinks. Fire spreads and spreads. None of them ever say: enough. I have what I need.

That is a picture of appetite without satisfaction. And it is a picture of what human desire looks like when it is pointed at the wrong things. We pursue possessions, status, experiences — and the hunger grows rather than shrinks. We get more and want more. It never says enough.

And then Jesus says this:

"Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they shall be satisfied." (Matthew 5:6)

Here is a hunger that actually gets satisfied. Not the endless appetite of Proverbs 30 — but a hunger that finds what it is looking for. Those who hunger and thirst for righteousness will be satisfied. The promise is certain.

The question is what we are hungering for. Because we are always hungering for something. The appetite is always there. The only variable is its object.

Hunger for righteousness. That is the hunger worth having.

To Him Be the Glory.
