Hungering and Thirsting

Joshua M. Haub · June 7, 2024

A helpful Proverb:

“The leech has two daughters: Give and Give. Three things are never satisfied; four never say, ‘Enough’: Sheol, the barren womb, the land never satisfied with water, and the fire that never says, ‘Enough.’” (Proverbs 30:15–16 ESV)

These are some of the teachings of Agur, son of Jakeh. One simple message: There are some things that are never satisfied with what they have; they always want more. The Leech had 2 greed daughters. There are also the 4 things that are never satisfied: Sheol, the barren womb, land, and fire.

Too often, I think I may fit into these 2 verses. As a young man who has been blessed with a lot of material possessions, it is easy to want more (or, in today’s terms, it is easy to want “the newest version” of things such as technology). As an American, my country does not help me to stop being like the leech’s daughters (Proverbs 30:15). Whatever I could possibly want, Amazon, or another website, will ship it straight to my house. In fact, if it does not arrive within 2 days (Don’t you just love Amazon Prime?), I may begin to get irritated or impatient. I love America. I am thankful to be here. But, I must learn to be content (Phil 4:11–13).

We all hunger for more, but let us learn to not hunger and thirst for material possessions that rot and decay, but for righteousness (Matt 5:6). If we hunger and thirst for righteousness, Jesus says we will be satisfied (Matt 5:6). What will you hunger for today?

To Him be the Glory.
