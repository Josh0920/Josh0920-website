Many Will Say to Me

Joshua M. Haub · November 11, 2023

Jesus closes the Sermon on the Mount with a series of warnings. The final one is the most sobering:

"Many will say to Me on that day, 'Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in Your name, and in Your name cast out demons, and in Your name perform many miracles?' And then I will declare to them, 'I never knew you; depart from Me, you who practice lawlessness.'" (Matthew 7:22–23)

Read that carefully. These are not atheists or obvious pagans. These are people who called Him Lord — twice. People who did things in His name. Prophesying, casting out demons, performing miracles. By any outward measure, they looked like they were in.

And Jesus says He never knew them.

The criterion He gives just before this is not activity in His name but doing the will of His Father:

"Not everyone who says to Me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of heaven, but he who does the will of My Father who is in heaven will enter." (Matthew 7:21)

Doing the will of the Father. Not performing impressive spiritual activities. Not religious productivity. Obedience — real, sustained, heart-level obedience to what God actually says.

The word many is the one that should stop us. Not a few confused people on the fringes. Many. A large number of people who were certain they were right with God and were not.

That is not a comfortable thought. It is not meant to be. It is meant to drive us to honest self-examination: am I doing the will of the Father, or am I doing things in His name while living for myself?

Are you one of the many?

To Him Be the Glory.
