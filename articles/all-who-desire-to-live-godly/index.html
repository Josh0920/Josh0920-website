All Who Desire to Live Godly

Joshua M. Haub · March 7, 2025

Paul writes to Timothy and points to his own life as evidence of something Timothy needs to understand:

"You, however, have followed my teaching, conduct, purpose, faith, patience, love, perseverance, persecutions, and sufferings — such as happened to me at Antioch, at Iconium and at Lystra; what persecutions I endured, and out of them all the Lord rescued me! Indeed, all who desire to live godly in Christ Jesus will be persecuted." (2 Timothy 3:10–12)

Paul names specific cities — Antioch, Iconium, Lystra. He is not speaking in abstractions. These were real places where real things happened to him. He was stoned at Lystra and left for dead. He is saying: Timothy, you know this. You saw some of it. And here is what it means.

All who desire to live godly in Christ Jesus will be persecuted.

Not "may be." Not "probably will be." Will be. The Greek is unambiguous. This is a promise, not a possibility.

That raises a question worth sitting with. If everyone who desires to live godly will be persecuted, and you are experiencing no friction with the world at all — what does that tell you? Either the world around you has become dramatically more godly, or you have accommodated yourself to it more than you realize.

This is not a call to manufacture conflict. It is a call to honest self-examination. Paul lived in a way that created friction. Jesus lived in a way that got Him killed. If our lives create no friction anywhere, we should ask why.

Desire to live godly. Expect what comes with it.

To Him Be the Glory.
