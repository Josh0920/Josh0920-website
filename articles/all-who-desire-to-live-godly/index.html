All Who Desire to Live Godly

Joshua M. Haub · March 7, 2025

As I have been studying through 2 Timothy, I’ve come across another passage that I believe all of us who are Christians ought to consider:

“Indeed, all who desire to live godly in Christ Jesus will be persecuted.” (2 Timothy 3:12, NASB1995)

It is important to recognize the tense of the verb: “will be.” That is an absolute. Notice, Paul didn’t say, “Indeed, all who desire to live godly in Christ Jesus ‘may’ be persecuted.” He didn’t even say, “Indeed, all who desire to live godly in Christ Jesus ‘probably’ will be persecuted.” He said “will be.”

Notice again the tense verb: “will.” Paul is saying that at some point during your life as a Christian, you will be persecuted. You may not walk outside your house everyday and have protestors throwing tomatoes at you, but there will be days you will, metaphorically. There will be days you are verbally or maybe even physically hurt because of your stand with Christ.

For those who claim to be Christians yet never endure persecution, Paul leaves 2 possibilities: either the world has become more godly or we have become more worldly. Which is more likely?

To God be the glory.
