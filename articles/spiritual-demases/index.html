Reflection

Spiritual Demases

Joshua M. Haub · March 18, 2023

Demas appears three times in Paul's letters. Trace the arc carefully — it is a warning.

First, in Colossians 4:14, he is mentioned alongside Luke as a fellow worker. He is in the company of Paul, doing the work.

Second, in Philemon 23, he is listed among Paul's companions — people who are laboring with him for the gospel.

Third, in 2 Timothy 4:10 — Paul's final letter, written from prison near the end of his life:

"Demas, having loved this present world, has deserted me and gone to Thessalonica." (2 Timothy 4:10)

Fellow worker. Companion. Gone. The whole arc in three verses.

Paul gives us the diagnosis: he loved this present world. Not a dramatic fall into obvious sin. Not a public scandal. Just a gradual reordering of affections — the world moved up, Christ moved down, until the cost of staying became greater than the pull of leaving.

Jesus described the same dynamic in the Parable of the Sower. The thorny soil receives the word — it even grows for a while — but the worries of the world and the deceitfulness of riches come up alongside and choke it out. The seed does not disappear in a day. It gets crowded out slowly, over time, by things that seem harmless individually but together leave no room for the word to bear fruit.

The sickness of selfishness kills the seed. It does not announce itself. It grows quietly until one day the person who was once a fellow worker is simply gone.

James 5:19–20 calls us to turn back those who stray before they are fully gone. Hebrews 12:1–2 calls us to lay aside every encumbrance and fix our eyes on Jesus. Both assume drift is possible — and that it can be reversed.

Are there areas of your life where the world is slowly crowding out the word?

To Him Be the Glory.

Joshua M. Haub · March 18, 2023
