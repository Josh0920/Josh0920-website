Walk Worthy of God’s Calling

Joshua M. Haub · February 20, 2026

There is a gap I keep running into. It is the gap between what I want to be and what I actually am. Between the kind of disciple I imagine myself to be and the one who shows up in real life — distracted, tired, slow to the Word, quick to everything else.

"I therefore, a prisoner for the Lord, urge you to walk in a manner worthy of the calling to which you have been called." (Ephesians 4:1)

Walk worthy. Not think worthy, not intend worthy, not feel worthy. Walk. The calling is real. The life has to match it.

Paul wrote those words from prison. He was not sitting in a comfortable room with a quiet morning and a full cup of coffee. He was chained to a Roman soldier. And from that place, he urged us to walk worthy.

I think about what it means to hunger for something. When you are truly hungry, you do not forget it. It is not one item among many on a list. It occupies you. You think about it. You make moves toward it.

What does it look like to hunger for God's Word that way? To treat it not as a discipline to maintain but as a need to satisfy? Job said he treasured the words of God's mouth more than his necessary food. I want that to be true of me. I am not always sure it is.

The calling is real. It was given by God, not earned by us. And exactly because it was given — exactly because it cost Him everything — it deserves to be walked out with everything we have.

Not when it is convenient. Not when we feel like it. Every day, in the ordinary moments, in the ones nobody sees.

Walk worthy.

To Him Be the Glory.
