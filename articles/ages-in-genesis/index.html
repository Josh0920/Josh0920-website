Notes

[1]
Sarah was 10 years younger (Gen 17:17) and she died at 127 (Gen 23:1). So 127 + 10 = 137.
[2]
Isaac was 40 when he married Rebekah (Gen 25:20), and Abraham was 100 when Isaac was born. So 100 + 40 = 140.
[3]
Abraham was 100 when Isaac was born. Isaac was 60 when his sons were born. So add 60 to 100.
[4]
Sarah was 10 years younger than Abraham (Gen 17:17). So subtract 10 from Abraham's age.
[5]
In Gen 17:17, Abraham is talking about how old he and Sarah would be when Isaac is born, not how old they are in the present time of ch. 17. In ch. 17, Abraham is 99 (Gen 17:1, 24). Also, notice how Ishmael is circumcised at 13, which lines up with how old Sarah was when he was born.
[6]
When Abraham was 99, Ishmael was 13. Isaac was born when Abraham was 100 (Gen 21:5), so add 1 year to 13.
[7]
Since Ishmael was 14 when Isaac was born, add 14 years to Isaac's age to find Ishmael's.
[8]
Abraham was 137 when Sarah died and lived 38 years after (died at 175). So add 38 years to 50 (Ishmael's age when Sarah died).
[9]
Sarah was 90 when Isaac was born and was 127 when she died. The difference is 37 years.
[10]
Abraham was 137 when Sarah died and lived for 38 more years (died at 175). Isaac was 37 when Sarah died, so add 38.
[11]
Since Isaac was 60 when he had Jacob and Esau, and Esau was 40 when he married these women (Gen 26:34), so add 60 + 40 = 100.
[12]
Since Ishmael was 14 years older than Isaac, take Ishmael's age of death and subtract 14.
[13]
Jacob had Joseph when he was 91 (which was the 14th year of his work for Laban). Isaac was 60 when Jacob was born, so take 91–14 (77) to find when Jacob arrived at Laban. Then add 60 to 77 (137). So Isaac was 137 when Jacob arrived at Laban, which is assumed to be the same year as the deception based on the language of the Genesis narrative.
[14]
Since Isaac was 60 when he had Jacob, once we find Jacob's age, we can just add 60 to find Isaac's.
[15]
To find how old Isaac was when Joseph was in Egypt (before he appeared before Pharaoh), take his age when he was sold (17) and the age he was when he stood before Pharaoh (30) — a 13-year window. Isaac was 151 when Joseph was born. Add 17 to get 168 (when Joseph was sold). From 168 for the next 13 years (181) Joseph is in slavery. However, Isaac dies at 180, so he dies 1 year before Joseph goes before Pharaoh.
[16]
Isaac was 151 when Joseph was born. Joseph appeared before Pharaoh when he was 30. Isaac would have been (had he lived) 181 when Joseph appeared before Pharaoh. Instead, he died 1 year before then, during the 2 years the cup-bearer had forgotten about Joseph. Thus 181 – 2 = 179.
[17]
Jacob/Esau were born when Isaac was 60, and Abraham died when they were 75 (Abraham was 100 when Isaac was born and died at 175). So 175 – 160 = 15.
[18]
Jacob/Esau were 63 when Ishmael died because Isaac is calculated to be 123 when Ishmael died. Since Isaac was 60 when Jacob/Esau were born, take 123 – 60 to find their age.
[19]
Jacob is 130 when he went to Egypt (Gen 47:9). This was when Joseph was 39. 130 – 39 = 91. Joseph was born in the 14th year of Jacob's work for Laban. Thus 91 – 14 = 77.
[20]
Jacob arrived at Laban when he was 77 and worked for 7 years to get his wives (Gen 29:18). Thus he was 84 when he married Leah and Rachel.
[21]
Based on the Genesis narrative, Jacob had Joseph in the 14th year of his work with Laban. When Joseph was born, Jacob wanted to leave, which is when Laban decided to give Jacob "wages" — lasting another 6 years (cf. Gen 30:25; 31:41). So 77 + 14 = 91.
[22]
Jacob arrived at Laban when he was 77 and worked for him for 20 years (Gen 31:41). So 77 + 20 = 97.
[23]
The exact age is unknown. Benjamin cannot be more than 6 years younger than Joseph, since Joseph was 6 when Jacob fled from Laban (and Benjamin was born sometime after that). So the youngest Jacob can be when Benjamin was born is 97.
[24]
Jacob/Esau were 91 when Joseph was born. Joseph was 17 when he was sold (Gen 37:2). So 91 + 17 = 108.
[25]
Take Jacob/Esau's age when Joseph was born (91), add 17 to get their age when he was sold into slavery (108). From 108 for the next 13 years (108–121), Joseph is in slavery.
[26]
Jacob/Esau were 121 when Joseph appeared before Pharaoh. Two years before that, Joseph interpreted the dreams of the cup-bearer and chief baker (Gen 41:1). So 121 – 2 = 119.
[27]
Isaac was 60 when Jacob/Esau were born and died at 180 (Gen 35:28). So 180 – 60 = Jacob/Esau's age (120).
[28]
To find Jacob's age in relation to Joseph's, take Joseph's age and subtract 91, since Joseph was born when Jacob was 91. Joseph was born in the 14th year of Jacob's work for Laban, which then became another 6 years. Total 20 years; Joseph born in year 14, so 20 – 14 = 6 years remaining when Joseph was born (cf. Gen 30:25; 31:41).
[29]
Joseph was 30 when he appeared before Pharaoh. It was 2 years before that when he interpreted the dreams of the cup-bearer and chief baker (Gen 41:1). Thus 30 – 2 = 28.
[30]
There is a 91-year age gap between Joseph and Jacob/Esau. Since Jacob/Esau were 120 when Isaac died, Joseph would have been 29 (120 – 91 = 29).
[31]
It is assumed here that the "7 years of plenty" started immediately after the visions were revealed. This assumption seems correct, seeing as the ages of Jacob (as indicated in this document) all line up perfectly with the dates specified in the Genesis narrative at the end of his life. Thus after these 7 years, Joseph would have been 37 (30 + 7 = 37).
[32]
Joseph was 30 when he appeared before Pharaoh. The "7 years of plenty" ended, then the "7 years of famine" began. In the 2nd year of famine, Joseph sent his brothers to get Jacob (Gen 45:6). Thus 30 + 7 + 2 = 39.
[33]
Joseph was 30 when he appeared before Pharaoh. There were then 7 years of plenty and 7 years of famine. So add 14 to 30 = 44.
[34]
Jacob was 91 when he had Joseph. Jacob died at 147. Thus 147 – 91 = 56. Alternatively: Joseph's age when Jacob arrived in Egypt (39) + 17 years Jacob lived in Egypt (Gen 47:28) = 56.
[35]
It is not possible based on Genesis to calculate the exact time when Benjamin was born. For purposes here, the "oldest" Benjamin could be is assumed: Jacob left Laban when Joseph was 6, and Benjamin was not born until after Jacob had fled (cf. Gen 35:16–19). Thus the smallest age gap there could be between Joseph and Benjamin is 6 years. A 6-year difference is assumed in this document.
[36]
To find Benjamin's age, take Joseph's age and subtract 6 years.
[37]
Since Benjamin is assumed to be 6 years younger than Joseph, Benjamin is 11 when Joseph is sold into slavery (17 – 6 = 11) and 24 when Joseph appears before Pharaoh (30 – 6 = 24).
[38]
Given the vague detail of time in Gen 41:50, it is impossible to give any precise dating for the births of Manasseh and Ephraim. The "oldest" possible ages are used here. For simplicity, both are assumed to be born in the same year (the first year of plenty, since that is the oldest they could possibly be). Their births had to occur during the 7 years of plenty, because Joseph did not marry Asenath until he stood before Pharaoh at 30 (Gen 41:45–46).
[39]
With the assumption that Manasseh and Ephraim were both born the same year (the first year of the years of plenty), subtract 30 years from Joseph's age to find their age (see note 31 on when the years of plenty started).
[40]
With the assumptions in notes 38 and 39, Manasseh and Ephraim are at the oldest possible age (born the first year of plenty). Thus Joseph would have been 30; to find Benjamin's age subtract 6 from Joseph's (see note 35); to find Jacob's age add 91 years to Joseph's age (Jacob had Joseph when he was 91, see notes 19–21).
