Why Am I Striving for Holiness?

Joshua M. Haub · July 1, 2023

Peter gives the clearest possible reason for pursuing holiness:

"As obedient children, do not be conformed to the former lusts which were yours in your ignorance, but like the Holy One who called you, be holy yourselves also in all your behavior; because it is written, 'You shall be holy, for I am holy.'" (1 Peter 1:14–16)

The reason is God. Not a preacher. Not parents. Not elders. Not tradition. Not social expectations. God is holy — therefore be holy. The motivation is His nature and His call, not the pressure of anyone around you.

This matters more than it might seem. The Corinthians were dividing themselves around men:

"I am of Paul," and "I of Apollos," and "I of Cephas," and "I of Christ." (1 Corinthians 1:12)

Paul rebuked them sharply. Has Christ been divided? Was Paul crucified for you? Their identity had become attached to human leaders rather than to God. And when your holiness is driven by loyalty to a person rather than devotion to God, it becomes fragile — it rises and falls with that person's influence.

John the Baptist understood this. When his disciples worried that Jesus was drawing bigger crowds, he said:

"He must increase, but I must decrease." (John 3:30)

A faithful teacher always points away from himself. The goal is never that people become devoted to the teacher — it is that they become devoted to God.

So before you strive for holiness, ask why. If the answer is God — His character, His call, the fact that He is holy — you have a foundation that cannot be shaken. If the answer is anyone else, it will not hold.

To Him Be the Glory.
