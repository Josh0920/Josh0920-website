WHY Am I Striving for Holiness?

Joshua M. Haub · July 1, 2023

WHY am I striving for Holiness and WHO do I follow?

The Apostle Peter wrote in his first letter:

“Because it is written, ‘You shall be holy, for I [God] am holy’” (1 Peter 1:16, NASB1995).

Peter in this section (verses 14–16) sheds light on a very important idea found in Christianity and the Old Testament. What he wrote is fairly easy to understand, but even more profound when examined next to what he did NOT say.

Peter did not write:
“Because it is written, ‘You shall be holy, for [your preacher is] holy’” (1 Peter 1:16, NASB1995).

Nor did he write:
“Because it is written, ‘You shall be holy, for [your parents are] holy’” (1 Peter 1:16, NASB1995).

Neither did he say:
“Because it is written, ‘You shall be holy, for [the elders are] holy’” (1 Peter 1:16, NASB1995).

Insert any spiritual influence you’d like there. Perhaps you could even put specific names. Of course, it is not wrong to have examples we look to as we strive to be holy; but, Peter’s point still stands:

“Because it is written, ‘You shall be holy, for I [GOD] am holy’” (1 Peter 1:16, NASB1995).

God must be our deepest motivator for why we want to be holy. We are not holy for attention, status, appreciation, pats on the back, positions, etc. We are not holy simply because others are. We are not even holy because we want to be like other “super spiritual people,” though it is good if their example motivates us. We are holy because YHWH is holy. We ought to want to be like YHWH, not a parent, preacher, elder, friend, etc.

Notice what Peter says just before verse 16:

“As obedient children, do not be conformed to the former lusts which were yours in your ignorance, but like the Holy One who called you, be holy yourselves also in all your behavior…”
— 1 Peter 1:14–15

We are called to be different because God is different. The idea of “Holiness” is to be “Set apart for a purpose.” We are God’s holy people. If we want to be God’s people, we must be like Him.

If we are trying to “be like” someone else, we are not truly “God’s people,” we are, instead, “[blank]’s people.” Our holiness (appealing to the definition above), then, is based on a different standard than that of Christ’s.

What I mean: Take the Corinthians. In 1 Corinthians 1:12, Paul writes:

“Now I mean this, that each one of you is saying, ‘I am of Paul,’ and ‘I of Apollos,’ and ‘I of Cephas,’ and ‘I of Christ.’”

The Corinthians were claiming to be disciples of other people instead of Christ. This caused division. Paul then says:

“Has Christ been divided? Paul was not crucified for you, was he? Or were you baptized in the name of Paul?” (1:13)

Paul concludes in verse 17 that his goal was to PREACH the GOSPEL, not baptize a lot of people. Paul was not converting people to some preacher, he was trying to get people to follow Jesus Christ. A lot of the Corinthians were not living as “God’s people,” but as “Paul’s disciples,” or “Apollos’ people.”

Thus, the Corinthians’ “holiness” (setting apart for a purpose) was then determined by what Paul, Apollos, or Cephas wrote instead of what CHRIST said.

Christians today (and those of the past, such as the Corinthians) are in grave danger of becoming the disciples of a source that is other than CHRIST and His Gospel. We have elevated preachers, elders, (man-made) traditions, etc into places only Christ ought to reign Supreme. Our allegiance is not to a name on a building, a preacher, or other source. Our allegiance is to Christ and Him Crucified.

The dangers of elevating “Other Sources”:

#1 — When our “Lord” and allegiance is in something other than Christ, we will inevitably leave the Way. We will listen to what [blank] says instead of what Christ says. Before long, the Bible’s authority is diminished as [blank]’s power rises.

#2 — We begin converting people to these “Other Sources” instead of to Christ. Too often, we’ve converted people to a specific preacher or Building Name instead of The Teacher.

#3 — Christ’s power, teaching, influence, and authority is thrown out the window. Thus, what is the point of the Bible? If I choose to follow [blank], why would I care what the Bible says? All my questions, concerns, and problems will not be filtered through the Scriptures or prayer, but through [blank]’s thoughts, ideas, and advice.

Our mindset will be the polar opposite of that of John the Baptist’s:

“He must increase, but I must decrease.”
— John 3:30

These are grave dangers. We must be very, very careful. Myself included.

To Him be the glory.
