Who Am I?

Joshua M. Haub · May 27, 2023

Two men in Scripture were elevated to positions of enormous power. Both had people beneath them who depended on them. Both were wronged by people close to them. And what they did with that power could not be more different.

Haman was given honor by King Ahasuerus — every official was commanded to bow before him. When Mordecai refused, Haman did not just nurse a personal grudge. He plotted to destroy every Jew in the entire kingdom. One man's refusal to bow became justification for genocide. Haman was full of himself, and he used his position to destroy everyone who threatened his pride.

Joseph was sold into slavery by his own brothers, falsely accused, and thrown into prison. Yet God raised him to be second only to Pharaoh over all of Egypt. When famine struck and his brothers came to him desperate and unknowing, Joseph did not take revenge. He wept. He provided. He forgave. And he said:

"As for you, you meant evil against me, but God meant it for good in order to bring about this present result, to preserve many people alive." (Genesis 50:20)

Same basic situation — a position of power over people who had wronged them. Haman used it to destroy. Joseph used it to save.

Everyone reading this has some kind of position — in a family, a workplace, a congregation, a community. People depend on you in some way. You have influence over someone.

The question is what you do with it. Are you a Haman or a Joseph?

Who are you?

To Him Be the Glory.

Joshua M. Haub · May 27, 2023
