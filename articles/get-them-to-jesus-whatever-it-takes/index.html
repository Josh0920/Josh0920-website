Reflection

Get Them To Jesus, Whatever It Takes

Joshua M. Haub · June 11, 2026

In Mark 2:1–12, there is a paralytic who is healed by Jesus. One of the more noteworthy things about the text is Jesus’ discussion with the scribes there. Jesus tells the man, “‘Son, your sins are forgiven’” (2:5) and the scribes began to reason and concluded, “‘He is blaspheming; who can forgive sins but God alone?’” (2:7). Jesus, then, shows them He can forgive sins by healing the paralytic. The man went out praising God and the crowds were amazed.

But notice how the paralytic got to Jesus in the first place:

“When He had come back to Capernaum several days afterward, it was heard that He was at home. And many were gathered together, so that there was no longer room, not even near the door; and He was speaking the word to them. And they came, bringing to Him a paralytic, carried by four men. Being unable to get to Him because of the crowd, they removed the roof above Him; and when they had dug an opening, they let down the pallet on which the paralytic was lying.” (Mark 2:1–4, NASB95)

The paralytic was brought to Jesus by four men. There are a few things that strike me about these four men.

1. They heard and thought

It seems that these four men probably heard that Jesus was in the area like everyone else in this scene. Maybe friends or family informed them, or they heard “the word on the street.” Everyone in the scene rushes to see Jesus. They want to see Him and hear Him teach. Interestingly, no healings are mentioned in verse 2. Instead, Mark writes that Jesus was speaking to them. It seems that this time, the people want to hear Him preach a message. So, everyone in this scene rushes to hear Jesus preach. I can imagine people fighting for a seat, a view, a place to stand in the doorway, anywhere as long as you could see and hear Jesus. However, these four men are different. The men do not rush to the house to find a seat. Instead, they thought about someone else: a paralytic. What was more important: getting a front row view or helping someone else get the help he needed? These four men give up what they could have gotten in order to help someone else.

2. They were persistent

The four men arrive at the house with the paralytic, but the crowd got there first. It is impossible for the men to get into the house, let alone bring in a paralyzed man on a bed! So, a new plan develops: destroy the roof and lower him down from above. A brilliant plan, but one that required a lot of effort. Firstly, the men had to get the paralyzed man onto the roof. That would not have been easy. I have no idea if there were stairs (which would have made it easier). Maybe there was a ladder or some other means to get up there. Next, the men had to break open the roof. Who knows how long that would have taken! Finally, they lowered the man down into the room. This operation was no easy task.

It would have been much easier for the men to give up and go home. If you cannot go in the front door, how else could you get in? I wonder how long it took them to come up with this plan. Who would have thought to destroy the roof?! I also wonder how many teachings of Jesus they “missed” because they were too busy creating and executing this operation. If I was there, I would not want to miss a single moment. Clearly this paralytic meant a lot to these four men. They desperately wanted Jesus to heal him. They were willing to do whatever it took—property damage and all.

3. They were selfless

I have alluded to this already, but I want to make it clearer. These men, when they heard Jesus was in town, could have rushed there themselves to see Him, but they did not. They got a paralytic, brought him to the house, found a way in, executed a masterful plan, helped the man get healed…and then what? The text does not say. Did they get a front row seat to the next sermon? Did they get praised by everyone for their good deed? Jesus, in Matthew’s account, “sees their faith” (Matt 9:2) but immediately directs His attention to the paralytic. The men are seemingly forgotten about while they are peering into the room from the roof! No praise, glory, etc.! But this story was not about them, nor did they see this as “their moment.” They had one purpose in coming to Jesus: get the paralytic healed. Mission accomplished. Now their purpose is done, and they fade into the background.

Their example. These four men are a great example of what we need to be doing: get sick people to Jesus. These men thought about someone else who needed Jesus. They saw the paralytic in his helpless state and refused to leave him to run to fight for a spot in the house to hear the most magnificent preacher. They carried him all the way there and then refused to give up trying even when the most convenient way in was not available. They did the impossible for him with no reward in sight for themselves. They knew Jesus was the answer to all his needs and they sought Him out desperately. Jesus does not come to one’s town often! They saw the opportunity and took it without a second thought. And what about us? What do the people we love need the most? A friend who is willing to do whatever it takes to get them the help they need. We must be desperate for our friends. Sin and salvation are serious. The opportunity of Jesus’ healing (forgiveness) will not be available forever. Jesus is the answer. Get them to Jesus, whatever it takes.

To Him be the Glory.
